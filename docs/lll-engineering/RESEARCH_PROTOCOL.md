## Purpose

Produce a research protocol and prior-art search plan for evaluating
whether Ladder–Lattice–Loop (LLL) Engineering can support a defensible,
useful, and complementary scholarly contribution.

This is NOT a manuscript. This is the protocol that governs any future
manuscript work.

## Research question

Can LLL Engineering contribute a defensible scholarly contribution
without overstating novelty, mathematical formality, empirical
validation, or disciplinary standing?

## Hypothesis (candidate, to be challenged)

> LLL Engineering may contribute a unifying recursive systems-engineering
> grammar that integrates governed progression, typed composition,
> runtime disposition, and evidentiary closure across technical,
> institutional, human, and agentic systems.

This hypothesis must be narrowed, challenged, and potentially rejected.

## Dispositions


## Prior-art search plan

### Search strategy

Search by underlying primitives, not only by the phrase "Ladder–Lattice–Loop."

### Nearest-neighbor families

At minimum, search these fields:

1. Institutional AI and public governance graphs
2. Proof-of-execution and runtime verification
3. Runtime assurance / Simplex architectures
4. Contract-based and assume-guarantee design
5. Authorization and delegation graphs
6. Risk lattices and policy refinement
7. Hierarchical state machines and transition systems
8. Graph transformation systems and graph grammars
9. Control theory and feedback systems
10. Cybernetics and viable-system approaches
11. Workflow nets, Petri nets, process algebras, temporal logics
12. Safety cases, assurance cases, evidence graphs
13. Organizational design, constitutional governance, institutional economics
14. Maturity models, stage-gate systems, promotion/demotion mechanisms
15. Provenance, event sourcing, audit logs, verifiable computation

### Search queries

For each family, run:

- Field name + "survey" OR "review"
- Field name + "formalism" OR "formal definition"
- Field name + "governance" OR "authority"
- Field name + "evidence" OR "receipt" OR "provenance"
- Field name + "composition" OR "lattice"
- Field name + "progression" OR "ladder" OR "stage-gate"
- Field name + "feedback" OR "loop" OR "closure"

### Inclusion/exclusion rules

**Include**:
- Peer-reviewed papers
- Established technical reports
- Foundational textbooks
- Standards documents
- Survey papers

**Exclude** (from primary prior art, but note separately):
- Blog posts (unless by established researchers)
- Marketing materials
- Unpublished preprints (note separately as preprints)
- AI-generated surveys (unless independently verified)

### Source quality requirements

- Retrieval date recorded for every source
- Canonical URL or DOI recorded
- Content hash for any locally stored content
- Evidence posture assigned: [VERIFIED], [OBS], [INF], [SEC], [GAP]

## Claim decomposition plan

Break LLL into atomic claims:

| Claim type | Examples | Evidence required |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| Descriptive | "LLL describes X" | Observation of X |
| Design principle | "LLL prescribes Y" | Argument + examples |
| Formal definition | "Lattice is a poset with joins/meets" | Mathematical proof |
| Invariant | "Authority propagates correctly" | Proof or exhaustive test |
| Novelty | "No prior framework combines these" | Prior-art map |
| Empirical | "LLL improves outcomes by Z%" | Controlled study |
| Normative | "Systems should use LLL" | Argument + evidence |
| Implementation | "LLL is implementable" | Working code |

## Formalism audit plan

Determine whether "lattice" is:

1. An informal multidimensional graph metaphor
2. A partially ordered set
3. A true order-theoretic lattice with joins and meets
4. A family of typed graphs with constrained authority propagation
5. Another formal object

**Rule**: Do not retain mathematical terminology for rhetorical force
if the corresponding structure is absent.

Determine whether LLL is best formalized as:

1. A labeled transition system
2. A graph transformation grammar
3. A category/compositional system
4. A contract system
5. A statechart or hierarchical state machine
6. A control/feedback architecture
7. A meta-model combining several of these
8. A non-formal engineering heuristic

**Acceptable outcome**: A non-formal but useful framework.

## Falsification plan

Develop cases where:

- Ladder, Lattice, and Loop add no value beyond existing methods
- The decomposition is ambiguous or non-unique
- Extra governance creates harmful friction
- Receipts create false confidence
- Authority models fail under distributed or adversarial conditions
- Progression is cyclic, continuous, or non-ordered rather than ladder-like
- Lattice composition creates contradictions
- Loop closure cannot be observed
- The framework merely redescribes systems after the fact

**Specify what evidence would weaken or reject LLL as a distinct contribution.**

## Case-study program plan

At least three heterogeneous cases:

1. `/loop` governed iteration and transition receipts
2. Constitutional-integrity remediation and amendment passage
3. A technical runtime, scientific workflow, or multi-agent system NOT designed using LLL

**Include at least one negative or low-fit case.**

**Case studies must not be presented as independent validation if
designed and evaluated only by the framework's originators.**

## Independent review plan

Separate reviewers required for:

- Prior art and novelty
- Mathematical/formal claims
- Systems-engineering usefulness
- Reproducibility and case-study method
- Reputational/public-communication risk

**Preserve unresolved objections.**

## Reproducibility packet plan

Preserve:

- Exact source corpus and retrieval dates
- Search queries and inclusion/exclusion rules
- Claim ledger
- Case-study inputs and evaluation rubrics
- Code/schemas/fixtures if formal validators are built
- Failed approaches and negative results
- Versioned manuscript and change history
- AI-use and contribution disclosures

## Reputational guardrails

Do not claim:

- That LLL is scientifically validated
- That no prior framework combines similar elements
- That the framework is mathematically formal unless definitions and proofs warrant that claim
- That case studies demonstrate generality without independent replication
- That arXiv posting equals peer review
- That HUMMBL has established a new academic discipline
- That memorable naming is evidence of novelty
- That agent-generated agreement is scholarly consensus

Public language should emphasize complementarity, bounded contribution,
limitations, and relationship to established fields.
