# Trust and Legitimacy Notes

Date: 2026-03-27
Status: draft

## Purpose

Capture why trust and legitimacy should be treated as design variables rather than vague outcomes.

## Useful Sources

### Trust, trustworthiness and AI governance

Source:

- <https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-024-71761-0>

What it contributes:

- trust in AI systems is not only about model quality
- institutional trustworthiness and governance matter directly

### Institutional trustworthiness and facial recognition acceptability

Source:

- <https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0740624X24000339>

What it contributes:

- public acceptability depends strongly on institutional trustworthiness
- technical capability alone is not sufficient for legitimacy

### Conceptualising Public Trust in High-Risk AI Policymaking

Source:

- <https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13876988.2025.2543546>

What it contributes:

- actor-specific trust matters
- trust in government and trust in companies should not be collapsed into one variable

## Why It Matters For HUMMBL

This strengthens the case for:

- BKI
- response readiness
- governance and capacity as separate from structural wickedness

In HUMMBL terms, legitimacy likely depends on:

- who chose the path
- whether the path can be inspected
- whether affected actors believe the process is fair and competent

## Candidate HUMMBL Dimensions

- institutional trustworthiness
- actor-specific legitimacy
- representation quality
- explainability adequacy by role
- evidence visibility

## Design Implication

HUMMBL should avoid assuming that trace visibility alone creates trust.

Trust appears to be:

- relational
- role-sensitive
- institution-mediated

That supports BKI as a serious companion layer, not a soft side note.
