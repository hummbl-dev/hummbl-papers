# Transdisciplinary Collaboration Notes

Date: 2026-03-27
Status: draft

## Purpose

Capture research relevant to collaboration across domains under uncertainty.

## Useful Sources

### Collaborative science-policy dialogues under uncertainty

Source:

- <https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/earth-science/articles/10.3389/feart.2025.1602005/full>

What it contributes:

- close collaboration does not remove uncertainty
- time windows, translation, and sustained dialogue matter

### Science and engineering for a world off track

Source:

- <https://council.science/publications/hlpf-2025-five-years-to-course-correct/>

What it contributes:

- inclusive and transdisciplinary collaboration is treated as necessary for SDG progress

### Knowledge integration in transdisciplinary research

Source:

- <https://theochli.github.io/assets/pdf/2025_pubjabi_knowledge_int.pdf>

What it contributes:

- knowledge integration has stages, mechanisms, and contextual dependencies
- collaboration quality depends on how knowledge is integrated, not just whether people are present

## Why It Matters For HUMMBL

This literature supports:

- BKI
- governance capacity
- coordination quality
- response-system readiness

It also suggests that some wickedness is experienced at the boundary between domains, not just within the problem domain itself.

## Candidate HUMMBL Takeaways

- add boundary-spanning as an explicit concern
- separate collaboration structure from collaboration quality
- treat knowledge integration as a process that can succeed or fail

## Design Implication

A refreshed framework may need to ask not only:

- how wicked is the problem?

But also:

- how hard is cross-boundary knowing and coordination in this case?
