# Change Management Adjacent Notes

Date: 2026-03-27
Status: draft

## Purpose

Capture change-management and implementation-science literature that is relevant to the Unified Tier Framework refresh.

The key question is not only:

- how wicked is the problem?

But also:

- how ready is the response system to execute change against that problem?

## Why This Matters For HUMMBL

The current tier framework mostly classifies the problem itself.

Recent change-management and implementation literature suggests that outcome quality is also heavily shaped by:

- readiness for change
- micro-level reception of change
- implementation climate
- coordination and relationships
- practical tailoring under resource constraints

This is directly relevant to the proposed separation between:

- structural wickedness
- response-system readiness

## Useful External Sources

### ORIC Psychometric Analysis (2024)

`A Psychometric Analysis of the Organizational Readiness for Implementing Change (ORIC) in a Child Welfare Setting`

Source:

- PubMed / DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1080/26408066.2024.2409092>

What it adds:

- readiness for change is measurable
- reliability and construct validity matter
- not every readiness instrument cleanly fits every context

Implication for HUMMBL:

- if HUMMBL adds a response-readiness layer, it should be treated as a measurable construct, not just a prose intuition

### Public HRO Change Reception (2024)

`Adapting to organizational change in a public sector high-reliability context: the role of negative affect and normative commitment to change`

Source:

- *Journal of Public Administration Research and Theory*
- <https://doi.org/10.1093/jopart/muae009>

What it adds:

- change reception and implementation dynamics at the micro level matter
- broad support/resistance measures are often too coarse

Implication for HUMMBL:

- the framework should not assume that governance or authority alone is enough
- actual change reception inside the implementing system matters too

### Sociotechnical Organizational Transformation Structuring (2024)

`Structuring and analyzing initiatives that facilitate organizational transformation processes: A sociotechnical approach`

Source:

- *Technological Forecasting and Social Change*
- ScienceDirect abstract page available locally via search result

What it adds:

- organizational transformation is multifactorial
- success depends on correlated initiatives rather than one variable at a time
- cognitive mapping and structural modeling are useful

Implication for HUMMBL:

- a profile or layered view is more defensible than a one-score answer
- change-management success factors may need to be modeled as interrelated, not additive only

### ART: Practical Tool for Implementation Tailoring (2024)

`The ART of Readiness: A Practical Tool for Implementation Tailoring at the Activity Level`

Source:

- Open-access PDF
- [PDF](https://www.wandersmancenter.org/uploads/1/2/8/5/128593635/gira_art_published_2024.pdf)

What it adds:

- practical tailoring under resource and time constraints
- readiness should be monitored through the intervention lifecycle
- intangible factors matter, including:
  - stakeholder mental models
  - implementation climate
  - interorganizational relationships

Implication for HUMMBL:

- response readiness should likely be iterative, not one-time
- stakeholder mental models and relationships are relevant to implementation tractability

### Change Management and Organizational Performance Review (2025)

`Change management and organizational performance: current key trends`

Source:

- Taylor & Francis review page

What it adds:

- digital transformation is shifting change-management practice
- more empirical work is still needed on direct performance effects

Implication for HUMMBL:

- keep claims disciplined
- frame a readiness or change layer as an operational aid first, empirical claim second

## Working Synthesis

The change-management literature supports a simple but important extension:

- wickedness describes the challenge
- readiness describes whether the organization can move against it

This suggests the Unified Tier Framework should eventually include a companion layer for:

- readiness
- implementation capacity
- coordination quality
- change reception

## Candidate HUMMBL Response-Readiness Dimensions

1. governance authority
2. implementation capacity
3. coordination capability
4. change readiness
5. stakeholder alignment for execution
6. implementation climate and trust

## Practical Next Step

Do not merge all of this into the wickedness score itself.

Instead:

1. preserve the wickedness score as a structural classifier
2. add a separate readiness or execution-capacity screen
3. test whether the combination predicts Base-N fit and intervention success better than wickedness alone
