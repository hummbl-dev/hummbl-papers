# Bio-Cognitive Readiness and BKI Note

Status: draft

## Purpose

Explain how the emerging bio-cognitive and bio-governance lane connects to the Unified Tier Framework rather than sitting outside it.

## Bottom Line

Bio-cognitive state should not be treated as a separate layer from the framework.

It belongs primarily under:

- `readiness`
- `BKI`

with only indirect effects on `wickedness`.

## Main Mapping

### Wickedness

Bio-cognitive factors rarely change the structural wickedness of the problem itself.

They can matter indirectly when:

- fatigue worsens coordination on already wicked problems
- overload increases premature closure
- poor recovery reduces adaptive capacity

But those effects are usually second-order.

### Readiness

This is the strongest fit.

Bio-cognitive state affects:

- execution capacity
- change efficacy
- coordination capability
- sustained operator performance
- ability to absorb workload without degradation

In other words, a system can be strategically ready on paper while still being practically unreadied by overload, fatigue, or strain.

### BKI

This is the second strongest fit.

Bio-cognitive conditions influence:

- interpretive alignment
- psychological safety
- knowledge-flow quality
- willingness to dissent
- ability to sustain shared attention

If people are overloaded or strained, the system may still produce data and discussion, but the quality of shared knowing degrades.

## Proposed Working Addition

When using the framework, consider a cross-cutting note:

- `bio-cognitive conditions`
  - a modifier on readiness and BKI, especially in long-horizon or high-load settings

This should not become a fourth top-level layer yet. It is better treated as a measurable modifier inside readiness and BKI.

## Example Signals

### Readiness-Adjacent

- sustained workload tolerance
- recovery opportunity
- fatigue accumulation
- ergonomic strain
- attention stability

### BKI-Adjacent

- willingness to speak up under load
- interpretive fragmentation during fatigue
- decreased trace usability under overload
- reduced cross-role translation quality

## HUMMBL Relevance

This gives the framework a more realistic account of why:

- high-wickedness situations sometimes fail because people are overloaded rather than conceptually confused
- lower-wickedness situations still collapse under weak operator readiness
- shared understanding degrades when human participants are exhausted or strained

## Working Rule

Do not treat operator state as a soft side issue.

In high-load human-AI systems, it is part of the execution environment.
