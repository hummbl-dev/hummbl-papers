# Sensemaking and Collective Interpretation Notes

Date: 2026-03-27
Status: draft

## Purpose

Capture why shared interpretation should matter to HUMMBL’s next framework layer.

## Working Observation

Many hard problems are not blocked only by lack of data.

They are blocked by:

- different interpretations of the same evidence
- breakdowns in shared framing
- inability to maintain a common picture while acting

## Useful Adjacent Source

### DASH: Deception-Augmented Shared Mental Model for Human-Machine Teaming

Source:

- <https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.18616>

What it contributes:

- shared mental models remain central in human-machine teaming
- mission resilience depends on maintaining coordinated interpretation

## Why It Matters For HUMMBL

This aligns with:

- BKI
- reasoning traces
- control regimes
- mission-governed coordination

The relevant question is not only whether a reasoning path exists.

It is whether multiple humans and agents can interpret the path coherently enough to coordinate around it.

## Candidate HUMMBL Dimension Family

- shared picture quality
- interpretive alignment
- disagreement legibility
- trace usability across roles
- common-intent stability

## Design Implication

This looks like a strong bridge concept between:

- wickedness
- readiness
- BKI

It may be one of the best arguments for keeping reasoning traces typed and role-aware rather than only verbose.
