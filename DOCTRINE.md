# DOCTRINE.md - hummbl-papers

**Status:** v0.1
**Steward:** HUMMBL Research Institute

## 1. Thesis

HUMMBL's governance infrastructure claims need external, peer-reviewable articulation to be credible beyond the operator's own mesh. hummbl-papers is the bet that working directories for arXiv submissions, with provenance-bearing citations and a submission checklist, turn internal R&D into scholarship that the broader AI governance community can engage with and build on.

The papers are not marketing. They are the formal articulation of HUMMBL's theoretical claims — Base120 as a governed mental-model layer, KRINEIA as sovereignty proof, BKI as belonging infrastructure — backed by citation lineages that trace each claim to its intellectual roots.

## 2. Conceptual vocabulary

- **Paper** — a working directory for an arXiv-bound manuscript.
- **Provenance** — the citation lineage tracing a claim to its intellectual source.
- **Submission checklist** — the pre-submission quality gate for paper-facing drafts.
- **Priority citation** — a foundational reference (Ashby, Turchin, Klein, Shannon) anchoring a claim.
- **Lineage** — the intellectual genealogy of a concept, from origin to HUMMBL articulation.

## 3. Design principles

1. Every claim carries provenance — no assertion without a citation lineage.
2. Submission checklist is mandatory before any draft goes to arXiv.
3. Papers are working directories, not final artifacts — drafts evolve in the open (within the org).
4. Priority citations anchor the theoretical warrant for each major claim.
5. Theory notes and paper drafts are review-based, not CI-validated, until mechanical checks apply.

## 4. Boundaries

hummbl-papers is NOT a blog, NOT a marketing surface, and NOT the canonical home for theory notes (that is hummbl-theory). It is the paper-production pipeline for formal scholarly articulation.
