## 2. Productized Consulting Packages

### The Three Flagship Packages

Productized consulting means fixed-scope, fixed-price offerings sold without a sales call. These are Reuben's highest-leverage plays because they can be sold from a landing page and delivered with a repeatable process.

#### Package 1: "AI Agent Security Audit" -- $5,000-$8,000

**What it is:** A 2-week assessment of a company's AI agent deployment for security, governance, and reliability gaps.

**Deliverables:**
- Architecture review of agent systems (tools, permissions, data access)
- Prompt injection and jailbreak vulnerability assessment
- Data leakage and PII exposure analysis
- Governance gap identification (logging, audit trails, human-in-the-loop)
- Risk-prioritized remediation roadmap (written report)
- 60-minute executive briefing

**Why it sells:** 85% of organizations have integrated AI agents into at least one workflow, but most have no governance framework. Fear of a data breach or regulatory penalty is a strong motivator.

**Why Reuben can deliver it:** HUMMBL's governance research translates directly into audit methodology. The capability security research from autoresearch reports provides the framework.

**Lead-to-HUMMBL pipeline:** Every audit finding is a potential HUMMBL feature validation. "Your agents lack X governance capability" becomes "HUMMBL provides X out of the box."

#### Package 2: "LLM Cost Optimization Review" -- $3,000-$5,000

**What it is:** A 1-week analysis of a company's LLM API spending with specific recommendations for cost reduction through model routing, local inference, caching, and prompt optimization.

**Deliverables:**
- Current spend analysis (API calls, token usage, model selection)
- Model routing recommendations (when to use GPT-4 vs Claude vs local models)
- Local inference feasibility assessment (for qualifying workloads)
- Prompt optimization for token reduction
- Caching strategy for repeated queries
- Projected 6-month savings estimate

**Why it sells:** Companies are spending $50K-$500K+/year on LLM APIs with no optimization strategy. A $3K review that saves $100K/year is an easy yes.

**Why Reuben can deliver it:** The local_vs_api_cost_analysis_2026 and llm_cost_optimization_2026 research reports are literally the methodology. The golden ratio stack benchmarks (133 tok/s local inference) provide real-world comparison data.

**Lead-to-product pipeline:** Companies that optimize locally need governance for their hybrid inference stack -- that is HUMMBL.

#### Package 3: "AI Governance Readiness Assessment" -- $8,000-$12,000

**What it is:** A 3-week evaluation of an organization's AI governance maturity against emerging regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001).

**Deliverables:**

## 3. Peptide Industry Consulting

## 4. Consulting as Lead Generation for Products

### The Flywheel Model

```
Consulting engagement
    -> Deep understanding of client pain points
    -> Validate/adjust product features
    -> Build case study (with permission)
    -> Client becomes early product user
    -> Case study attracts next consulting client
    -> Repeat
```

### Every Engagement is Market Research

| Consulting Package | Product It Validates | Data Collected |
|---|---|---|
| AI Agent Security Audit | HUMMBL governance features | Real-world agent architectures, common vulnerabilities |
| LLM Cost Optimization | HUMMBL model routing | Actual API spending patterns, workload profiles |
| AI Governance Assessment | HUMMBL compliance modules | Regulatory gaps, enterprise requirements |
| Peptide Compliance Audit | Peptide-checker | Quality verification workflows, testing gaps |
| Telehealth Platform Review | Peptide-checker | Distribution channel needs, integration points |

### Building Case Studies from Consulting

**The process:**
1. Include case study permission in the consulting contract (anonymized by default)
2. Document the "before state" at engagement start
3. Track specific metrics improved during engagement
4. Write up the transformation: problem -> approach -> result -> ongoing need
5. Use the case study in three places: landing page, LinkedIn content, sales calls

**Case study template:**
- "Company X was spending $Y/month on LLM APIs with no governance framework. After our 2-week optimization review, they reduced costs by Z% and implemented agent-level access controls. They now use [HUMMBL/peptide-checker] for ongoing monitoring."

### IP and Non-Compete Considerations

**Protect yourself:**
- Consulting contract must state that all pre-existing IP (HUMMBL, peptide-checker, autoresearch) remains yours
- Work product created during engagement belongs to client unless it overlaps with your product IP
- Avoid non-compete clauses. If a client insists, narrow the scope: specific industry vertical, specific geography, limited duration (6 months max)
- Use a "tools and methodology" carve-out: "Consultant retains ownership of all proprietary tools, frameworks, and methodologies used in delivery"
- Never build custom software for a client that competes with your own products. Consult and advise; do not build their version of your product.

### Converting Clients to Product Users

**The natural progression:**
1. Consulting identifies a specific, ongoing need (e.g., "you need continuous agent monitoring")
2. You solve it manually during the engagement
3. You show them the product that automates it: "I built this tool that does what I just did for you, but continuously"
4. Offer a pilot/beta at a discount as part of the consulting wrap-up
5. Transition from consulting retainer to product subscription + lighter advisory

**Key insight from the research:** 71% of B2B buyers are more likely to engage with consultants who tailor messages to their specific industry. The consulting relationship provides the ultimate tailoring -- you already know their exact problems.

### How to Raise Rates Over Time

**Month 1-3: $150/hr (market entry)**
- Accept any reasonable engagement to build portfolio
- Focus on collecting testimonials and case studies
- Use platforms (Toptal, A.Team) at lower rates to fill pipeline

**Month 3-6: $200/hr (specialization premium)**
- Publish 2-3 case studies showing measurable results
- Shift from hourly to fixed-price packages (which implicitly raise your effective rate)
- Drop or raise rates on platform work; shift to direct clients

**Month 6-12: $250-$300/hr (expert positioning)**
- Reference specific ROI delivered to past clients
- Offer only productized packages (no open-ended hourly)
- Develop a waitlist -- perceived scarcity drives rate acceptance
- Introduce retainer-only availability for new clients

**Rate-raising signals (when the market is telling you to charge more):**
- You are turning away work
- Clients accept your price without negotiation
- You are fully booked 3+ weeks out
- Referrals are coming in without outreach
- Competitors with less expertise charge more

### When to Stop Consulting

The exit trigger matrix:

| Product Revenue | Consulting Action |
|---|---|
| $0-$3K MRR | Consult 10 hrs/week, product work is evenings/weekends |
| $3K-$8K MRR | Reduce to 5-7 hrs/week consulting, shift to advisory retainers |
| $8K-$15K MRR | Reduce to 2-3 hrs/week, only high-value retainers |
| $15K+ MRR | Stop consulting except strategic advisory at $300+/hr |

**The decision framework:** If an hour spent on product development generates more long-term value than an hour of consulting revenue, shift the hour to product. But consulting at $250/hr while product revenue is $0 is not optional -- it is survival.

### Protecting Product Development Time

- **Block product time first.** Put 4 hours/day of product development on the calendar before accepting any consulting work. Consulting fills the remaining slots.
- **Batch consulting days.** Tuesdays and Thursdays are consulting days. Monday/Wednesday/Friday are product days. This prevents context-switching tax.
- **No same-day turnarounds.** Set expectations that deliverable timelines are measured in business days, not hours. This protects your product schedule.

## 7. Tax Optimization for Consulting Income

### Consulting Through the LLC

All consulting revenue should flow through HUMMBL's existing LLC structure (assuming it is set up, or a personal LLC).

**Benefits:**
- Liability protection for consulting engagements
- Clean separation of business and personal finances
- All business expenses deductible (home office, equipment, software subscriptions, AI API costs)
- Pass-through taxation (profits flow to personal return)

### Quarterly Estimated Payments

As a sole proprietor or single-member LLC, you owe:
- Federal income tax (marginal rate, likely 22-32% bracket for consulting income)
- Self-employment tax: 15.3% on first $176,100 (2025 cap), 2.9% above that
- State income tax (varies)

**Payment schedule:** April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
**Rule of thumb:** Set aside 30-35% of every payment received into a separate tax savings account

### Business Expense Deductions

Legitimate deductions for AI/ML consulting:

| Expense | Approximate Annual Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Home office (dedicated space) | $1,500-$5,000 | Simplified method: $5/sq ft, max 300 sq ft |
| Computer equipment (desktop, GPU) | Actual cost | 100% bonus depreciation in 2026 (permanent per OBBBA) |
| AI subscriptions (Claude, GPT, etc.) | $2,000-$5,000 | Fully deductible as business tools |
| Cloud compute (API costs) | $1,000-$3,000 | Direct cost of service delivery |
| Software subscriptions | $1,000-$2,000 | IDE, project management, invoicing |
| Professional development | $500-$2,000 | Conferences, courses, books |
| Internet (business %) | $600-$1,200 | Proportional to business use |
| Professional liability insurance | $500-$1,500 | Errors & omissions coverage -- get this |
| Business travel | Variable | Client site visits, conferences |

### S-Corp Election: Timing and Decision Framework

**The S-Corp question:** When consulting profit (not revenue -- profit after deductions) exceeds approximately $80K-$100K consistently, an S-Corp election can save on self-employment taxes by splitting income into salary + distributions.

**How it works:**
- LLC files Form 2553 to elect S-Corp tax treatment
- You pay yourself a "reasonable salary" (subject to payroll taxes)
- Remaining profit distributed as shareholder distribution (no self-employment tax)
- You still pay income tax on everything

**Savings by income level:**

| Consulting Profit | Salary | Distribution | Approx Annual Tax Savings | Net After Admin Costs |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $80,000 | $55,000 | $25,000 | ~$3,186 | ~$936 after $2,250 admin |
| $130,000 | $75,000 | $55,000 | ~$5,500 | ~$3,000-$4,000 |
| $150,000 | $80,000 | $70,000 | ~$8,954 | ~$6,704 |
| $250,000 | $110,000 | $140,000 | ~$10,031 | ~$7,781 |

**Admin costs of S-Corp:**
- Payroll service: $500-$1,500/year
- Additional tax prep (Form 1120-S): $1,000-$4,000/year
- Total overhead: $1,500-$5,500/year

**Reuben's decision timing:**
- At $78K/year (10 hrs x $150): Do NOT elect S-Corp. Savings are marginal after admin costs.
- At $130K/year (10 hrs x $250): CONSIDER S-Corp. Net savings ~$3K-$4K/year. Worth it if you have a good CPA.
- At $150K+ year: ELECT S-Corp. Net savings $5K+ justify the admin overhead.

**Critical 2026 deadline:** Form 2553 must be filed by March 15, 2026 for 2026 tax year treatment. Since today is March 24, 2026, you would need to file for a late election or target January 1, 2027. Talk to a CPA about late election relief under Rev Proc 2013-30.

**Important Social Security trade-off:** Lower salary through S-Corp means lower Social Security credits. At $150K profit with $80K salary, you would record ~$58K less in Social Security earnings, potentially reducing future benefits by ~$1,500/month. For a younger founder, this may be worth the current tax savings, but it is worth understanding.

## 9. Risk Mitigation

### Time Risk: Consulting Consumes Product Development Time

**Mitigation:** Hard cap at 10 hours/week. Block product time first, fill remaining slots with consulting. Batch consulting to 2 days/week maximum.

### Reputation Risk: Bad Engagement Damages Brand

**Mitigation:** Scope every engagement tightly. Use fixed-price packages (not open-ended hourly) so both sides know what "done" looks like. Walk away from scope creep.

### IP Risk: Client Claims Ownership of Your Ideas

**Mitigation:** Iron-clad IP clause in every contract. Pre-existing IP (HUMMBL, peptide-checker, all autoresearch work) stays yours. Period.

### Dependency Risk: One Big Client = Single Point of Failure

**Mitigation:** No single client should exceed 40% of consulting revenue. Diversify across AI consulting and peptide consulting. Retainers > project work for revenue stability.

### Tax Risk: Underpayment Penalties

**Mitigation:** Set aside 30-35% of every payment immediately. File quarterly estimates. Get a CPA before the first quarterly payment is due (April 15).
