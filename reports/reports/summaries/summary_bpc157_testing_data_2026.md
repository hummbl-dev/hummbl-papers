# Summary: RQ-PEP-001 v2 -- BPC-157 Testing Data Survey (Expanded)

**Source:** bpc157_testing_data_2026.md | **Date:** 2026-03-23

- **Finnrick dataset expanded to 450 samples / 68 vendors** (up from 444/65). New A-rated vendors identified: PepTime and Yabang Peptide. Peptide Sciences -- previously A-rated -- ceased all operations March 6, 2026, removing one of the largest gray-market vendors.
- **Aspartimide isomer risk deepened:** Sigma-Aldrich documentation identifies up to nine distinct by-products from a single aspartimide event at BPC-157's Asp-Asp motif, none detectable by mass spectrometry. This is the largest uncharacterized quality risk in the BPC-157 market.
- **Three salt forms now tracked (acetate, TFA, arginine):** Salt form significantly affects actual peptide content per milligram. TFA content can reach 35% of gross weight. Expanded COA verification framework added with specific red flags for fabricated documents.
- **MZ Biolabs added as third testing lab** (DEA Schedule III licensed, Arizona-based), used by vendors including Accelerate Labs and Sports Technology Labs. Janoshik public test data confirms 99.3-99.9% purity from top-tier vendors (Jan-Mar 2026).
- **HHS reclassification context integrated:** Kennedy's Feb 27 announcement that BPC-157 will move to Category 1 creates demand before quality infrastructure exists. Formal FDA list not yet published.
