# Summary: RQ-PEP-004 -- Peptide Stability and Degradation

**Source:** RQ-PEP-004_stability.md | **Date:** 2026-03-15

- **Lyophilized peptides are far more stable than reconstituted:** All four peptides studied (BPC-157, semaglutide, TB-500, CJC-1295) last 2+ years lyophilized at -20C but only 2-6 weeks reconstituted at 2-8C. Moisture control matters more than temperature for sealed vials.
- **Each peptide has unique vulnerabilities:** BPC-157 is unusually stable in gastric acid (enabling oral delivery) but light-sensitive. Semaglutide must never be frozen (destroys efficacy) and is humidity-sensitive. TB-500 loses 2-5% per freeze-thaw cycle. CJC-1295 with DAC has dramatically extended in-vivo half-life (6-8 days vs 30 minutes without DAC).
- **Bacteriostatic water extends reconstituted life to 4-6 weeks** vs only 1-2 weeks with sterile water. The 0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative inhibits microbial growth. Sterile water should only be used for single-dose preparations.
- **Visual degradation signs:** Cloudiness = bacterial growth or aggregation; yellowing = irreversible oxidation; wet/clumped lyophilized powder = moisture ingress through compromised seal. Any of these warrant discarding the product.
- **Cold chain is critical for reconstituted solutions but less so for sealed lyophilized vials:** Brief heat exposure during shipping (4-6 hours) is unlikely to cause meaningful degradation in properly lyophilized products because the absence of water prevents most degradation reactions.
