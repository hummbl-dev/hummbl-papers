# Summary: RQ-PEP-003 v2 -- Consumer Peptide Testing Methods (Comprehensive)

**Source:** peptide_testing_methods_2026.md | **Date:** 2026-03-23

- **Pricing landscape expanded:** Finnrick now offers free basic HPLC testing. Peptide Test has a $7 promotional rate. TruLab at $200. Full panel testing (HPLC + LC-MS + endotoxin + sterility) runs $828-1,158 at Janoshik. Prices continue trending downward as competition increases.
- **HPLC validation metrics quantified:** LOD ~0.05-0.1% of total peptide content; accuracy recovery 98-102%; RSD under 2.0% repeatability. Key limitation reiterated: HPLC alone cannot confirm which peptide is present -- MS confirmation is essential for identity verification.
- **CLIA certification is NOT required** for non-clinical peptide analytical testing (not diagnosing human disease). ISO 17025 accreditation is the relevant standard. This is significant for anyone considering offering testing services.
- **Emerging technologies are 5-10 years out:** Aptamer-based biosensors, paper microfluidics, and AI-enhanced spectroscopy show promise but none are commercially available for peptide-specific consumer applications today.
- **No viable at-home peptide test exists:** Visual inspection, reconstitution clarity, and COA verification remain the only zero-cost consumer options, but they cannot detect wrong peptide, sub-potency, or contamination. The global peptide synthesis market has reached $1.9B (projected $2.59B by 2031).
