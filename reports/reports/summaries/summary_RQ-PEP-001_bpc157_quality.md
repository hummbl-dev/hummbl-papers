# Summary: RQ-PEP-001 -- BPC-157 Quality Landscape

**Source:** RQ-PEP-001_bpc157_quality.md | **Date:** 2026-03-15

- **Vendor quality is highly variable:** Finnrick Analytics tested 444 samples across 65 vendors; purity ranged 96-99%+ but quantity accuracy deviated up to +/-75% from labels. Only ~5 vendors earned top "A" ratings (Peptide Partners, Peptide Sciences, Eternal Peptides).
- **Contamination is widespread:** ~8% of gray-market samples showed endotoxin contamination; a Drug Testing and Analysis study found 30% of online peptides had incorrect amino acid sequences and 65% had unsafe endotoxin levels. USADA reported 20%+ mislabeling rate.
- **BPC-157's Asp-Asp motif is a unique quality risk:** Adjacent aspartic acid residues at positions 10-11 are vulnerable to aspartimide formation during synthesis and deamidation after reconstitution. Mass spectrometry cannot distinguish these isomers from correct BPC-157.
- **Price correlates loosely with quality:** Vendors below $25/5mg are high-risk; premium vendors ($50-60/5mg) generally deliver better results, but high price alone does not guarantee quality. 10mg vials offer best per-mg value from reputable sources.
- **FDA Category 2 classification** prevents licensed compounding pharmacies from compounding BPC-157 for human use, pushing consumers to the unregulated gray market where quality control is inconsistent.
