# Summary: RQ-PEP-002 (Extended) -- GLP-1 FDA Enforcement Deep Dive

**Source:** glp1_fda_enforcement_2026.md | **Date:** 2026-03-23

- **JMIR study details are stark:** Three online semaglutide vendors (SemaSpace, USChemLabs, BiotechPeptides) delivered product at 7.7-14.4% purity vs 99% claimed, with ALL samples containing endotoxin. Three other vendors (GeniusPharmacy, PureMedsOnline, WeightCrunchShop) never delivered product at all -- pure e-commerce scams. Content exceeded labels by 29-39%, creating overdose risk.
- **FAERS pharmacovigilance data shows compounded GLP-1s are dramatically more dangerous:** Compounded products showed 48.9x higher reporting odds for preparation errors, 19x for contamination, and 8.5x for compounding/manufacturing issues vs FDA-approved versions. 605 adverse events for compounded semaglutide alone by July 2025.
- **Novo Nordisk's litigation-to-partnership strategy set industry precedent:** Sued Hims & Hers in February 2026 over Patent 8,129,343, then settled in one month with a collaboration deal where Hims sells branded Novo products. Novo's $24.9B semaglutide revenue (2024) drives aggressive enforcement. Composition of matter patents expire December 2031.
- **Price gap is the root driver:** Branded GLP-1s cost $1,300-1,900/month vs $150-500 for compounded versions (75-90% savings). ~80% of post-shortage compounded prescriptions now include supplemental ingredients (B vitamins) to argue they are not "essential copies" -- a legal strategy of uncertain validity.
- **Salt form matters for safety:** Compounders using semaglutide sodium and semaglutide acetate instead of semaglutide base creates unknown pharmacokinetic differences. This is a testable quality marker for a verification service.
