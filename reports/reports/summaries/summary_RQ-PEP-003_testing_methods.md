# Summary: RQ-PEP-003 -- Consumer Peptide Testing Methods

**Source:** RQ-PEP-003_testing_methods.md | **Date:** 2026-03-15

- **LC-MS at $200/sample is the best consumer option:** Combined HPLC + mass spectrometry from labs like ZyntroTest and TruLab provides both purity percentage and molecular identity confirmation with 3-5 day turnaround. Prices have dropped from $500+ to $200 as competition increased.
- **Handheld Raman spectroscopy is not consumer-viable:** Entry-level devices cost $10,000-$30,000, lack peptide-specific reference libraries, and cannot determine purity. No devices exist in a consumer-accessible price range.
- **Consumer NIR devices (SCiO, ~$300-500) are useless for peptides:** They can detect "protein bonds" generically but cannot identify specific peptides or measure purity. Resolution is fundamentally insufficient for peptide-level analysis.
- **No rapid test strips exist for peptides:** Lateral flow immunoassays (like COVID rapid tests) have not been developed for research peptides. Each peptide would need unique antibodies/aptamers, and market size does not justify development cost.
- **Key COA red flags for consumers:** COAs from unknown labs, no lot number matching product, purity numbers without chromatograms, and reuse of the same COA across multiple batches all indicate unreliable vendors.
