# Summary: RQ-PEP-002 -- The GLP-1 Compounding Crisis

**Source:** RQ-PEP-002_glp1_crisis.md | **Date:** 2026-03-15

- **Unprecedented FDA enforcement:** 50+ warning letters to compounders (Sep 2025) and 30 to telehealth companies (Mar 2026) -- exceeding the prior decade's total. Import Alert 66-80 ("Green List") established to detain non-approved GLP-1 APIs at the border.
- **Compounded product quality is dangerous:** Novo Nordisk testing found compounded semaglutide with impurities up to 86%, dosing from 0.4% to 302% of labeled strength. A JMIR study found online-sold semaglutide at only 7-14% purity with 100% endotoxin contamination rate.
- **Significant consumer harm documented:** 900+ adverse events, 17 deaths, 100+ hospitalizations reported in FDA FAERS. Root causes include dosing errors from multi-dose vials, contamination, wrong substances, and products with near-zero active ingredient.
- **Counterfeit branded products entered the legitimate supply chain:** Fake Ozempic pens (lots PAR0362 and PAR1229) were seized in April and December 2025. Primary source countries: China, Turkey, India. 38 state AGs petitioned FDA for action.
- **Market resolved through landmark Novo/Hims settlement (Mar 2026):** Hims will sell branded Ozempic/Wegovy and cease compounded GLP-1 advertising. With semaglutide and tirzepatide off shortage lists, the legal basis for most GLP-1 compounding has ended.
