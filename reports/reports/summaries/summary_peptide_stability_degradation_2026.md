# Summary: RQ-PEP-004 v2 -- Peptide Stability and Degradation (Expanded)

**Source:** peptide_stability_degradation_2026.md | **Date:** 2026-03-23

- **Expanded to cover Ipamorelin and PT-141 (Bremelanotide)** in addition to original four peptides (BPC-157, semaglutide, TB-500, CJC-1295). Incorporates 2025 forced-degradation studies for semaglutide that characterize D-amino acid isomers (D-Ser, D-His, D-Asp) as key degradation products.
- **Three most impactful storage interventions beyond refrigeration:** pH management (keep solutions at pH 7.0-7.8 for most peptides), nitrogen-purged headspace to displace oxygen, and amber glass or foil wrapping for light protection. Each needle puncture introduces cumulative oxidative stress.
- **Quantitative degradation data added:** Asp-Xxx peptide bonds are ~100x more labile than other residues (relevant to BPC-157's Asp-Asp motif). Asn-Gly deamidation hotspot reacts up to 70x faster than Asn with bulky residues. Aggregation kinetics now characterized as pH-dependent.
- **CJC-1295 DAC linker chemistry detailed:** Maleimide hydrolysis can release the DAC moiety, reducing the extended half-life that is the entire point of the DAC modification.
- **Container-closure leachable risks documented:** Rubber stoppers and certain vial materials can leach extractables into reconstituted solutions, adding a degradation pathway beyond the peptide's own chemistry.
