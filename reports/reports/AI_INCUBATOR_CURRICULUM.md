## 1. Existing Incubator Landscape

### 1.1 Y Combinator (3-Month Batch)

**Deal:** $500K total investment. $125K converts to a fixed 7% equity stake; $375K via uncapped MFN SAFE that converts at terms of the next priced round. No fees charged.

**Structure (11 weeks):**
- **Week 0:** 3-day in-person kickoff in San Francisco. Orientation, cohort bonding, partner introductions.
- **Weeks 1-4:** Heads-down building. Group office hours every two weeks with YC partners. One-on-one office hours available on demand. Focus: get to product-market fit or die trying.
- **Weeks 5-8:** Weekly dinners with guest speakers (founders, VCs, executives). Continued building and iteration. Peer accountability via batch-mates.
- **Weeks 9-10:** Demo Day preparation. Pitch refinement, story crafting, investor targeting.
- **Week 11:** Demo Day. Presentations to curated audience of investors and press.

**What YC actually provides:**
- The alumni network (most cited value by far -- Stripe, Airbnb, Dropbox founders accessible)
- Brand signal to investors (YC acceptance = instant credibility)
- Peer pressure from high-caliber batch-mates
- Direct intros to Series A investors
- Bookface internal platform for founder-to-founder help

**Key stats (2025-2026):** ~60% of YC companies are now AI-focused. Four batches per year. Hundreds of companies per batch.

### 1.2 Techstars (13-Week Program)

**Deal:** $220K total. $20K via Post-Money Convertible Equity Agreement (5% common stock) + $200K uncapped MFN SAFE.

**Three-Phase Structure:**

| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Key Activities |
|-------|-------|-------|----------------|
| **1. Mentor Madness** | 1-4 | Network building | Meet ~100 mentors in rapid-fire sessions. Select 3-5 lead mentors as pseudo board of advisors. Absorb feedback on product, market, strategy. |
| **2. Execution** | 5-8 | Build and validate | Apply mentor feedback. Hit KPIs. Work closely with managing directors. Launch prototypes, acquire initial customers, prove traction. |
| **3. Demo Day Prep** | 9-13 | Storytelling and fundraising | Pitch refinement, deck design, investor collateral. Masterclasses on specific topics. Culminates in Demo Day pitch to investors. |

**Weekly anchors:** One-on-one with managing director + all-hands KPI meeting. Otherwise, schedule is intentionally unstructured.

**Additional programming:** Workshops, masterclasses, speaker series, pitch practice. Topics: hiring, marketing, product, fundraising. Founder story sessions from serial entrepreneurs.

### 1.3 On Deck (Community-Based Model)

**Deal:** ~$2K fee, no equity taken.

**Structure (8-10 weeks, fully virtual):**
- Buffet-style workshops (non-technical hiring, consumer subscriptions landscape, etc.)
- Sessions with industry experts
- Coworking sessions and virtual dinners
- Mastermind groups of 6 people (matched by timezone + experience), meeting every other week for 90-minute video calls
- Live masterclasses, small-group deal labs, fireside chats with GPs

**Model philosophy:** Community-led education. The network IS the product. Expanded from Founders fellowship (2016) into dozens of programs: VCs, angel investors, community managers, podcasters, fintech, healthtech, climate tech.

**Key insight:** On Deck proved that community + lightweight structure can create value without large capital commitment or equity dilution.

### 1.4 F/ai -- The Multi-Lab AI Accelerator (Launched January 2026)

**Deal:** $1M+ in credits, no equity. Backed jointly by OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta, Microsoft, Mistral AI, plus Hugging Face, Sequoia, and General Catalyst.

**Structure:** 2 batches of 20 companies per year. Paris-based. Targets AI-native startups with demonstrated revenue potential (threshold: ~EUR 1M revenue).

**Significance:** Validates the thesis that AI companies need AI-specific acceleration, not generic startup advice.

### 1.5 What Founders Say Is Most vs. Least Valuable

**Most Valuable (consistent across all programs):**

| Rank | Value | Why It Matters |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Alumni network** | Warm intros, shared knowledge, pattern-matching from founders who've done it |
| 2 | **Brand credibility** | YC/Techstars badge opens investor doors immediately |
| 3 | **Peer accountability** | Batch-mates create healthy pressure to ship |
| 4 | **Investor access** | Demo Day + partner intros compress fundraising timeline |
| 5 | **Specific tactical mentorship** | Mentors with domain expertise who've built similar companies |

**Least Valuable / Most Complained About:**

| Issue | Frequency | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Generic mentorship** | Very common | Mentors without founder experience giving textbook advice. "Primarily lecture-based seminars, generic pitch coaching, or outsourced mentorship with little founder time." |
| **Mentor time-wasting** | Common | Meeting 100 mentors in Month 1 (Techstars) consumes enormous founder time. Many meetings produce nothing actionable. |
| **One-size-fits-all curriculum** | Common | SaaS company and biotech company get the same workshops. Irrelevant content wastes time. |
| **Equity cost for program quality** | Moderate | 5-7% equity is expensive if the program itself (vs. the brand) delivers limited value. |
| **Lack of follow-through** | Moderate | Mentors participate in a couple of calls, maybe attend Demo Day, then disappear. No sustained engagement. |
| **Intensity mismatch** | Moderate | Lightning pace may be premature for founders still validating their idea. |

**The gap this curriculum fills:** Founders want specific, actionable, domain-relevant help -- not generic workshops. They want 24/7 availability, not scheduled office hours. They want research done FOR them, not another lecture about how to do research.

## 2. AI-Native Curriculum (12 Weeks)

### Design Principles

1. **AI does the grunt work, founders make decisions.** Every overnight research session produces artifacts the founder reviews in the morning -- not homework they have to do themselves.
2. **Output-oriented, not attendance-oriented.** Each two-week sprint produces a specific deliverable. No workshops for workshop's sake.
3. **Domain-specific by default.** Healthtech founders get healthcare compliance research. Fintech founders get financial regulation analysis. The AI adapts; the curriculum doesn't force conformity.
4. **Solo-founder compatible.** Designed for 1-2 person teams. No activities that assume you have a 5-person founding team.

### Curriculum Overview

```
Week  1-2:  DISCOVERY        -> Validated idea + market map
Week  3-4:  ARCHITECTURE     -> Technical spec + sprint plan
Week  5-6:  BUILD            -> Working MVP
Week  7-8:  LEGAL/COMPLIANCE -> Entity formed, launch-ready
Week  9-10: GO-TO-MARKET     -> GTM plan + launch materials
Week 11-12: LAUNCH/REVENUE   -> Live product + first revenue
```

### Week 1-2: Discovery

**Goal:** Transform a rough idea into a validated opportunity with evidence.

**AI-Powered Activities:**

| Day | Activity | AI Role | Founder Role |
|-----|----------|---------|--------------|
| Day 1 | Kickoff intake session | Structured interview to capture idea, assumptions, target market | Answer questions, share context |
| Night 1 | **Overnight market analysis** | Autonomous research session generates 15-25 page market report: TAM/SAM/SOM, growth trends, regulatory landscape | Sleep |
| Day 2 | Market report review | Highlight key findings, flag risks | Read, annotate, challenge assumptions |
| Night 2 | **Competitor landscape** | Deep analysis of 20-50 competitors: pricing, features, positioning, funding history, tech stack, reviews | Sleep |
| Day 3-4 | Competitor review + gap analysis | Generate positioning matrix, identify white space | Validate, prioritize differentiators |
| Night 3-4 | **Customer research synthesis** | Scrape reviews, forum posts, social media for customer pain points. Synthesize into demand signals. | Define customer segments to investigate |
| Day 5-7 | Problem-solution fit workshop | Generate hypotheses, design validation experiments | Conduct 10-15 customer discovery calls (human-only) |
| Night 5-7 | **Interview synthesis** | Analyze call transcripts, extract patterns, update market thesis | Record and upload call notes |
| Day 8-14 | Validation sprint | Refine all research based on customer feedback. Generate final market map. | Make go/no-go decision on idea direction |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Market Analysis Report (15-25 pages, sourced)
- [ ] Competitor Landscape Matrix (20-50 companies analyzed)
- [ ] Customer Pain Point Synthesis
- [ ] Problem-Solution Fit Assessment (scored)
- [ ] **Validated Idea Brief** (2-page executive summary of opportunity)

**Human mentor touchpoint:** 1-hour session with domain expert to pressure-test market thesis.

### Week 3-4: Architecture

**Goal:** Turn the validated idea into a buildable technical specification.

**AI-Powered Activities:**

| Day | Activity | AI Role | Founder Role |
|-----|----------|---------|--------------|
| Day 1 | Architecture intake | Assess founder's technical capability, existing tools, budget constraints | Describe technical comfort level and constraints |
| Night 1 | **Technical architecture session** | Generate full architecture spec (modeled on HUMMBL_ARCHITECTURE_SPEC pattern): system overview, data model, API design, infrastructure plan, cost projections | Sleep |
| Day 2-3 | Architecture review | Walk through spec, explain tradeoffs, answer questions | Challenge assumptions, flag concerns |
| Night 2-3 | **Stack selection analysis** | Decision matrix comparing frameworks, hosting, databases, payment processors. Scored on: cost, speed-to-ship, scalability, solo-founder maintainability. | Define weighting priorities |
| Day 4-5 | Stack decision + data model | Refine based on founder decisions | Make final stack choices |
| Night 4-5 | **MVP feature prioritization** | Generate feature list from market research. Score each on: customer value, build effort, revenue impact, differentiation. Produce MoSCoW prioritization. | Review and adjust priorities |
| Day 6-10 | Sprint planning | Break MVP into 2-week sprints. Generate user stories, acceptance criteria, task breakdown. | Approve sprint scope |
| Day 11-14 | Dev environment setup | Generate boilerplate, CI/CD config, deployment scripts, monitoring setup | Follow setup guide, verify everything works |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Technical Architecture Specification (full document)
- [ ] Stack Selection Decision Matrix (scored)
- [ ] Data Model + API Design (ERD + endpoint documentation)
- [ ] MVP Feature Prioritization (MoSCoW matrix)
- [ ] **Sprint 1 Plan** (user stories, tasks, acceptance criteria)
- [ ] Dev Environment (repo, CI/CD, staging deployment)

**Human mentor touchpoint:** 1-hour session with senior engineer to review architecture decisions.

### Week 5-6: Build

**Goal:** Ship a working MVP with core functionality and payment integration.

**AI-Powered Activities:**

| Day | Activity | AI Role | Founder Role |
|-----|----------|---------|--------------|
| Daily | **AI-assisted coding** | Claude Code / Cursor as pair programmer. Generate code from specs, write tests, debug, refactor. | Write code alongside AI, review all generated code, make product decisions |
| Daily | **Morning standup** (15 min) | Review yesterday's commits, flag code quality issues, suggest today's priorities based on sprint plan | Report blockers, adjust priorities |
| Daily | **Evening code review** | Automated PR review: security, performance, best practices, test coverage | Merge or revise based on feedback |
| Week 5, Day 3-5 | **Payments integration** | Generate Stripe integration code, webhook handlers, subscription logic, billing portal | Configure Stripe account, test payment flows |
| Week 6, Day 1-3 | **Auth + user management** | Implement auth flow, user roles, data privacy controls | Define access rules, test edge cases |
| Week 6, Day 4-7 | **MVP hardening** | Load testing, security audit, error handling, logging, monitoring | Fix critical issues, deploy to staging |

**Daily rhythm:**
```
08:00  Morning standup with AI review (15 min)
08:30  Coding session 1 (AI pair programming)
12:00  Lunch break
13:00  Coding session 2 (AI pair programming)
17:00  Evening code review (AI-generated PR review)
17:30  Tomorrow planning (AI suggests next tasks from sprint backlog)
```

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Working MVP deployed to staging
- [ ] Stripe payments integration (subscriptions or one-time)
- [ ] User authentication and authorization
- [ ] Core feature set (3-5 features from MoSCoW "Must Have")
- [ ] Test suite (>70% coverage on critical paths)
- [ ] **Production deployment** (live URL, SSL, monitoring)

**Human mentor touchpoint:** 2 sessions -- mid-sprint code review + end-of-sprint product review.

### Week 7-8: Legal and Compliance

**Goal:** Form legal entity, handle regulatory requirements, become launch-ready.

**AI-Powered Activities:**

| Day | Activity | AI Role | Founder Role |
|-----|----------|---------|--------------|
| Night 1 | **Overnight legal research** | Entity structure analysis: LLC vs C-Corp vs S-Corp for this specific business, state-by-state comparison, tax implications, investor compatibility | Sleep |
| Day 1-2 | Entity decision + formation | Generate formation documents, operating agreement drafts, registered agent options | Review, make entity decision, file paperwork |
| Night 2 | **Insurance research** | Analyze required insurance by industry: E&O, general liability, cyber liability, D&O. Compare providers and pricing. | Define risk tolerance |
| Day 3-4 | Insurance procurement | Generate applications, comparison matrix | Select and purchase policies |
| Night 3-4 | **Domain-specific regulatory analysis** | Deep dive into industry regulations. Healthtech: HIPAA, FDA, state telehealth laws. Fintech: money transmitter licenses, KYC/AML. SaaS: GDPR, CCPA, SOC2. | Define compliance scope |
| Day 5-7 | Compliance implementation | Generate privacy policy, terms of service, cookie consent, data processing agreements. For regulated industries: compliance checklists, required documentation. | Review all legal documents, engage attorney for final review |
| Day 8-10 | **Contract templates** | Generate customer contracts, vendor agreements, NDA templates, contractor agreements tailored to this business | Review and customize |
| Day 11-14 | IP protection | Trademark search, patent landscape analysis, trade secret documentation | File trademark applications, document IP |

**Deliverables:**
- [ ] Legal entity formed (EIN obtained, bank account opened)
- [ ] Operating agreement / bylaws
- [ ] Insurance policies in place
- [ ] Regulatory Compliance Report (domain-specific)
- [ ] Privacy policy, terms of service, cookie policy (live on site)
- [ ] Contract templates (customer, vendor, contractor, NDA)
- [ ] IP protection filings initiated
- [ ] **Compliance Checklist** (all items green for launch)

**Human mentor touchpoint:** 1-hour session with startup attorney for final legal review. (NOTE: AI generates drafts; attorney reviews. This saves 10-20 hours of billable time.)

**Critical disclaimer:** All legal documents are AI-generated drafts. Founders MUST have an attorney review before finalizing. The incubator provides attorney referrals but does not provide legal advice.

**Goal:** Execute launch, acquire first customers, generate first revenue.

**AI-Powered Activities:**

## 3. Mentor Network

### 3.1 AI Agent Mentors (Always-On)

The incubator deploys specialized AI agents as domain-specific mentors available 24/7:

| Agent | Domain | Capabilities | Tools |
|-------|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Market Analyst** | Market research | TAM/SAM/SOM, competitor analysis, trend identification, demand signal detection | Web search, financial databases, social listening |
| **Technical Architect** | Engineering | Architecture review, stack selection, code review, security audit, performance optimization | Claude Code, static analysis, dependency auditing |
| **Legal Researcher** | Legal/compliance | Entity formation guidance, regulatory research, contract drafting, IP landscape analysis | Legal databases, regulatory APIs, template libraries |
| **Growth Strategist** | Marketing/GTM | SEO strategy, content generation, email sequences, community playbooks, launch planning | SEO tools, email platforms, social analytics |
| **Financial Modeler** | Finance | Unit economics, pricing optimization, fundraising prep, financial projections | Spreadsheet generation, benchmark databases |
| **Healthcare Specialist** | Healthtech | HIPAA compliance, FDA pathways, CMS coverage analysis, clinical trial landscape, NPI/provider data | Healthcare MCP tools (CMS Coverage, Clinical Trials, ICD-10, NPI Registry, ChEMBL) |

**Key advantage over traditional mentorship:**
- Available at 2 AM when the founder is stuck
- Never gives generic advice -- always researched, always specific to the company
- Produces written artifacts, not verbal hand-waving
- Zero ego, zero agenda, zero calendar conflicts

### 3.2 Human Mentors (High-Touch, Scheduled)

AI cannot replace humans for certain critical functions:

| Function | Why Human Required | Frequency |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Fundraising coaching** | Investors invest in people, not decks. Reading the room, building rapport, negotiating terms -- these are human skills. | 2x during program + on-demand during raise |
| **Hiring and team building** | Culture, chemistry, and leadership assessment are deeply human. | 1x during program + on-demand |
| **Emotional support** | Founder loneliness, imposter syndrome, burnout, co-founder conflict. AI can listen, but founders need someone who's been there. | On-demand (peer group + dedicated coach) |
| **Domain expertise validation** | AI generates research; domain experts validate it. A cardiologist reviewing a healthtech product. A securities lawyer reviewing a fintech compliance plan. | 2-3x during program (matched to relevant phase) |
| **Investor introductions** | Warm intros still outperform cold outreach by 10x+. This requires human networks. | End of program / Demo Day |

**Mentor compensation:** $2,000-5,000 per cohort commitment (3-5 sessions per company). Domain experts on hourly retainer ($200-500/hr, capped at 3 hours per company).

### 3.3 Healthcare MCP Integration (for Healthtech Founders)

Founders building in healthcare get access to specialized MCP tools already integrated into the AI mentor layer:

- **CMS Coverage Analysis** -- Search national and local coverage determinations, check Medicare reimbursement eligibility
- **Clinical Trials Search** -- Competitive intelligence on what's in the pipeline, endpoint analysis, investigator identification
- **ICD-10 Code Lookup** -- Validate diagnosis/procedure coding for billing integration
- **NPI Registry** -- Provider verification, specialty network building, referral pathway mapping
- **ChEMBL** -- Drug compound analysis, bioactivity data, mechanism of action research (for biotech/pharma founders)

This is a concrete differentiator: no other incubator provides programmatic access to healthcare regulatory databases as part of the curriculum.

## 4. Community Component

### 4.1 Cohort Structure

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Cohort size** | 5-10 companies per batch |
| **Batch frequency** | 4x per year (quarterly) |
| **Selection criteria** | Solo founders or 2-person teams. Must have a defined problem and target customer. Technical ability or willingness to learn. No "idea-stage only" -- must have done at least basic customer discovery. |
| **Diversity requirement** | Mix of industries per cohort (not all SaaS, not all healthtech). Geographic diversity encouraged. |

### 4.2 Weekly Rituals

| Day | Ritual | Format | Duration |
|-----|--------|--------|----------|
| **Monday** | Sprint kickoff | Async (each founder posts weekly goals in Slack) | 10 min |
| **Wednesday** | Cohort standup | Live video call, each founder: 2 min update (wins, blockers, asks) | 30 min |
| **Friday** | Demo Day | Live video, 1 founder presents progress (rotating), cohort gives feedback | 60 min |

**Friday Demo Day rotation:** Each founder presents every 2-3 weeks. Presentations evolve with the curriculum:
- Weeks 1-2: Present market thesis
- Weeks 3-4: Present architecture decisions
- Weeks 5-6: Live product demo
- Weeks 7-8: Compliance readiness review
- Weeks 9-10: GTM strategy pitch
- Weeks 11-12: Investor pitch rehearsal

### 4.3 Communication Infrastructure

| Channel | Platform | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Main cohort channel** | Slack or Discord | Daily communication, wins, asks, memes |
| **Domain channels** | Slack or Discord | #healthtech, #fintech, #saas, #devtools -- cross-cohort, permanent |
| **1:1 founder matching** | Monthly random pairing | Two founders from different cohorts connect for 30-min call |
| **Resource library** | Notion or similar | All AI-generated research reports, templates, playbooks (searchable) |
| **Alumni channel** | Slack or Discord | Graduates stay connected. Cross-referrals, hiring, advice. |

### 4.4 Alumni Network

- All AI research reports are archived and searchable by future cohorts (anonymized where needed)
- Alumni get continued access to AI mentor agents at reduced rates
- Annual alumni summit (virtual or in-person)
- Alumni founders serve as guest mentors for new cohorts (paid)
- Deal flow sharing: alumni who become investors get first look at new cohort companies

- Architecture phase: ~10 sessions = ~$280

### 5.2 Revenue Models

**Option A: Fee-Based (No Equity)**

| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|----------------|
| **Standard** | $10,000 | Full 12-week curriculum, AI mentor access, community, 3 human mentor sessions |
| **Premium** | $20,000 | Standard + unlimited human mentor sessions, dedicated domain expert, priority AI queue, attorney-reviewed legal docs |

Revenue per cohort (5 companies, mixed): $50,000-100,000
Margin: 29-60%

**Option B: Equity-Based (YC Model)**

| Structure | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Investment | $25,000-50,000 |
| Equity | 3-5% |
| Expected value | If 1 in 10 companies reaches $10M valuation, 4% equity = $400K return per success |

Revenue per cohort: $25K-50K cash + equity portfolio
Long-term value: Significant if cohort quality is high

**Option C: Hybrid (Recommended)**

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Program fee | $5,000 (covers direct costs) |
| Equity | 2-3% (aligns incentives) |

Revenue per cohort: $25,000 cash + equity in 5 companies
Break-even: Achieved on cash basis at 5 companies
Upside: Entirely in the equity portfolio

### 5.3 Break-Even Analysis

**Scenario: Hybrid model, $5K fee + 2% equity, 5 companies per cohort**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Cash revenue per cohort | $25,000 |
| Cost per cohort (lean) | $40,000-50,000 |
| Cash deficit per cohort | ($15,000-25,000) |
| Cohorts per year | 4 |
| Annual cash deficit | ($60,000-100,000) |
| Companies per year | 20 |
| Equity held per year | 2% in 20 companies |

**Path to profitability:**
