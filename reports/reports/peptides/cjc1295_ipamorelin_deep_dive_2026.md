## Executive Summary

CJC-1295/Ipamorelin is the most widely used growth hormone secretagogue stack in the peptide community. It combines two complementary pathways -- a GHRH analog (CJC-1295) that extends GH pulse duration with a ghrelin mimetic (Ipamorelin) that amplifies pulse magnitude -- producing synergistic GH elevation approximately 3-8x above baseline, roughly 20-30% higher than either compound alone. Despite its popularity (~110K combined monthly US searches), the evidence base is thin: two Phase I trials for CJC-1295 DAC (halted after a patient death attributed to pre-existing coronary disease), two completed Phase II trials for ipamorelin (for post-operative ileus, not anti-aging), and zero controlled human trials of the combination for the indications consumers actually seek (fat loss, muscle gain, anti-aging, sleep improvement). Both compounds were removed from the FDA Category 2 restricted list in September 2024 but remain in regulatory limbo -- not on Category 1, not FDA-approved, and currently restricted for compounding. WADA prohibits both under the S2 class. The market operates in a gray zone where telemedicine clinics charge $285-675/month for protocols built on mechanistic reasoning rather than direct clinical proof.

## 1. CJC-1295: Mechanism and Pharmacology

### What It Is

CJC-1295 is a synthetic analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH), specifically a modified version of the first 29 amino acids of GHRH (GHRH 1-29). It was developed by ConjuChem Technologies (Montreal, Canada) in the mid-2000s. The peptide incorporates four amino acid substitutions at positions 2, 8, 15, and 27 that confer:

- **Position 2:** Resistance to dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4) degradation
- **Position 8:** Reduced asparagine rearrangement and amide hydrolysis
- **Position 15:** Enhanced bioactivity
- **Position 27:** Prevention of methionine oxidation

### Mechanism of Action

CJC-1295 selectively binds to and activates GHRH receptors on anterior pituitary somatotroph cells, initiating:

1. Enhanced adenylyl cyclase activation and cyclic AMP production
2. Increased protein kinase A (PKA) phosphorylation cascades
3. Stimulation of growth hormone gene transcription and protein synthesis
4. Pulsatile growth hormone release that mimics natural physiological patterns

Key distinction: CJC-1295 increases GH secretion while **preserving pulsatility** -- it does not flatten the natural GH pulse pattern the way exogenous GH administration does.

### CJC-1295 DAC vs. No-DAC (Mod GRF 1-29)

| Property | CJC-1295 with DAC | CJC-1295 without DAC (Mod GRF 1-29) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Half-life | 6-8 days | ~30 minutes |
| Dosing frequency | 1-2x per week | 2-3x daily |
| GH release pattern | Sustained, continuous elevation | Pulsatile, mimics natural rhythm |
| Albumin binding | Covalent (via maleimidopropionic acid linker to Cys-34 on albumin) | None |
| Molecular weight | ~3,900 Da (with DAC) | ~3,367.9 Da |
| Half-life extension mechanism | ~1,000x over native GHRH via albumin hitchhiking (albumin t1/2 ~19 days) | DPP-4 resistant substitutions only |
| Clinical preference | Less common in anti-aging protocols | Preferred by clinics for more physiologic pulsatile GH release |

**The DAC (Drug Affinity Complex)** is a maleimidopropionic acid linker covalently attached to the lysine residue at position 27. Upon subcutaneous injection, this linker reacts with the free thiol group of cysteine residue 34 on serum albumin, forming a stable covalent bond that extends circulation time dramatically.

### Published Clinical Data

**Phase I (Teichman et al., 2006, JCEM):**
- Two randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind, ascending-dose trials in healthy adults (ages 21-61)
- Single injection of CJC-1295 DAC produced dose-dependent increases:
  - Mean plasma GH: 2-10x baseline elevation, sustained for 6+ days
  - Mean plasma IGF-1: 1.5-3x baseline elevation, sustained for 9-11 days
- After multiple doses: mean IGF-1 levels remained above baseline for up to 28 days
- Estimated half-life: 5.8-8.1 days
- Side effects: mild injection site reactions, flushing, water retention, insomnia
- "Safe and relatively well tolerated, particularly at doses of 30 or 60 mcg/kg"

**Phase I (Ionescu & Walker, 2006, JCEM):**
- Confirmed preserved GH pulsatility during continuous stimulation
- CJC-1295 increased trough GH, mean GH, and IGF-1 with maintained pulse architecture

**Phase II (ConjuChem, 2006 -- DISCONTINUED):**
- 12-week study of CJC-1295 DAC in 192 HIV patients with visceral adiposity (lipodystrophy)
- Multicentre, randomized, placebo-controlled, double-blind
- **Halted after patient death** (see Safety section below)

**Serum Proteomics Study (Sam & Bhangoo, 2006, Growth Factors):**
- CJC-1295 activation of the GH/IGF-1 axis produced measurable serum protein profile changes in normal adult subjects

### The Canadian Adverse Event Case

In 2006, a participant in the ConjuChem Phase II lipodystrophy trial died of acute myocardial infarction approximately 2 hours after receiving their 11th weekly dose of CJC-1295 DAC. The patient experienced chest discomfort, ECG confirmed acute MI, and death occurred approximately 1 hour later.

**The attending physician's assessment:** The most likely explanation was pre-existing asymptomatic coronary artery disease with plaque rupture and occlusion, unrelated to CJC-1295 treatment.

**However:** The trial was halted as a precaution, and ConjuChem never resumed development. No subsequent sponsor has advanced CJC-1295 DAC through clinical trials for any indication.

**FDA's position:** The FDA has flagged CJC-1295 for heart-related side effects including increased heart rate, flushing, and systemic vasodilatory reaction. They have also raised immunogenicity concerns, noting potential for anaphylaxis due to the peptide's non-endogenous structure.

### Evidence Quality Assessment

| Claimed Benefit | Evidence Level | Notes |
|----------------|---------------|-------|
| GH/IGF-1 elevation | **Strong** (Phase I data) | 2-10x GH, 1.5-3x IGF-1 reliably demonstrated |

## 2. Ipamorelin: Mechanism and Pharmacology

### What It Is

Ipamorelin is a synthetic pentapeptide (Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2) developed by Novo Nordisk in the late 1990s. It was the first growth hormone secretagogue to demonstrate selective GH release without significant effects on other pituitary hormones.

### Mechanism of Action

Ipamorelin is a ghrelin mimetic that selectively activates the growth hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R1a) on pituitary somatotroph cells. It initiates GH release through a pathway complementary to GHRH:

- **GHRH pathway (CJC-1295):** Stimulates via cAMP/PKA signaling
- **Ghrelin/GHS-R pathway (Ipamorelin):** Stimulates via phospholipase C/IP3/calcium signaling

### The Selectivity Advantage

Ipamorelin's defining characteristic is its selectivity for GH release, with minimal effects on other hormones. This is the primary reason it was called "the first selective growth hormone secretagogue" (Raun et al., 1998):

| Hormone | Ipamorelin Effect | GHRP-6 Effect | GHRP-2 Effect |
|---------|------------------|---------------|---------------|
| Growth Hormone | Strong stimulation | Strong stimulation | Strong stimulation |
| ACTH/Cortisol | No significant change (even at >200x ED50) | Significant increase | Moderate increase |
| Prolactin | No significant change | Significant increase | Moderate increase |
| FSH, LH, TSH | No change | Variable effects | Variable effects |
| Appetite/Hunger | Minimal | Strong increase (ghrelin-like) | Moderate increase |

This selectivity profile means ipamorelin causes fewer side effects than earlier GH secretagogues -- no cortisol spikes (which cause stress/anxiety/muscle breakdown), no prolactin elevation (which can cause gynecomastia in men), and minimal appetite stimulation.

### Published Clinical Data

**Raun et al. (1998, European Journal of Endocrinology):**
- Landmark paper establishing ipamorelin as the first selective GHS
- Demonstrated GH release potency comparable to GHRP-6 without cortisol or prolactin elevation
- Selectivity maintained even at doses >200x the ED50 for GH release

**PK/PD Modeling (Gobburu et al., 1999, Pharmaceutical Research):**
- Pharmacokinetic-pharmacodynamic modeling in human volunteers
- Characterized the dose-response relationship for GH release

**Post-Operative Ileus Trials (Helsinn Therapeutics):**

- **NCT00672074 (Phase II, 2008-2009):** 117 patients, double-blind, placebo-controlled. Ipamorelin 0.03 mg/kg twice daily for up to 7 days was well tolerated. However, no significant differences vs. placebo on primary efficacy endpoints.

- **NCT01280344 (Phase II, 2011-2014):** 320 patients across 45 US sites, dose-finding study for post-operative GI recovery after bowel resection. Completed but did not advance to Phase III.

**Growth Hormone Secretagogue Review (Ishida et al., 2020, JCSM Rapid Communications):**
- Comprehensive review of GHS history, mechanism, and clinical development
- Positions ipamorelin within the broader GHS landscape

### Safety Profile

**Reported adverse events (from clinical trials and post-market use):**
- Mild injection site reactions (pain, redness)
- Transient hunger/appetite increase (minimal compared to GHRP-6)
- Occasional headache or dizziness (mild, self-limiting)
- Flushing
- Water retention

**Serious risks flagged by FDA:**
- Immunogenicity (non-endogenous peptide structure; potential for anaphylaxis)
- Intravenous administration carries elevated risk (subcutaneous is standard)
- No long-term safety data beyond weeks-long clinical trials

## 3. The CJC/Ipa Stack: Why This Combination

### Synergistic Mechanism

The CJC-1295/Ipamorelin combination exploits two independent GH release pathways:

1. **CJC-1295 (GHRH analog):** Increases the number of somatotroph cells that respond to a GH stimulus ("primes the pump")
2. **Ipamorelin (GHS-R agonist):** Amplifies the magnitude of the GH pulse from each responding cell ("hits the trigger")

Together: more cells releasing more GH per pulse. Research protocols using both compounds consistently show IGF-1 elevation approximately 20-30% higher than either compound alone at equivalent doses.

The combination produces 3-8x baseline GH elevation, compared to 2-4x for either compound used individually.

### Why This Specific Pairing

- **Ipamorelin over GHRP-6/GHRP-2:** No cortisol spike, no prolactin, minimal hunger
- **CJC-1295 (no DAC) over DAC version:** More physiologic pulsatile release, pairs better with daily ipamorelin dosing
- **Together over exogenous GH:** Preserves natural pulsatility, lower cost, potentially fewer side effects, lower legal risk (schedule-wise)

### Common Dosing Protocols

**Protocol A: CJC-1295 no-DAC + Ipamorelin (Most Common Clinical Protocol)**

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| CJC-1295 (no DAC) dose | 100-200 mcg per injection |
| Ipamorelin dose | 100-300 mcg per injection (start at 100, titrate to 200-300) |
| Frequency | 1-3x daily, most commonly once at bedtime |
| Timing | Before bed (aligns with natural nocturnal GH surge), ideally 2+ hours after eating |
| Administration | Subcutaneous injection (belly, thigh, or upper arm) |
| Cycle length | 3 months on, 1 month off (prevents receptor desensitization) |
| Total annual cycles | 3 cycles per year |

**Protocol B: CJC-1295 DAC + Ipamorelin (Less Common)**

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| CJC-1295 DAC dose | 1-2 mg once weekly |
| Ipamorelin dose | 100-300 mcg daily |
| Duration | 8-16 weeks |
| Rationale | DAC provides sustained baseline GH elevation; ipamorelin adds daily pulse on top |

**Reconstitution:** Both peptides are supplied as lyophilized powder, reconstituted with bacteriostatic water (0.9% benzyl alcohol).

## 5. Quality and Testing

### CJC-1295 DAC Stability: The Linker Problem

The DAC (Drug Affinity Complex) is a maleimidopropionic acid linker designed to bind albumin in vivo. However, this same reactive linker creates stability challenges in vitro:

**Maleimide hydrolysis:** The maleimido group can undergo hydrolysis (ring-opening) in aqueous solution, converting to a non-reactive form that cannot bind albumin. This effectively converts CJC-1295 DAC into a compound that retains the DAC molecular weight but has lost its half-life extension mechanism.

**Stability data (from RQ-PEP-004 and industry reports):**

| Condition | Estimated DAC Linker Integrity |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Lyophilized, -20C | >95% at 24 months |
| Lyophilized, 2-8C | >90% at 12 months |
| Lyophilized, 25C | **>15% conversion reported at 6 months** |
| Reconstituted, 2-8C | Weeks before significant degradation |
| Reconstituted, 25C | Days to weeks |

The >15% conversion at 25C/6 months finding means that CJC-1295 DAC stored at room temperature for extended periods may deliver a product where a meaningful fraction of the active compound has lost its ability to bind albumin, effectively behaving more like a short-acting CJC-1295 (no-DAC) product.

### Common Quality Issues

**1. DAC vs. No-DAC Mislabeling**

This is the single most consequential quality issue in the CJC-1295 market. The two compounds have fundamentally different dosing protocols:

- DAC: 1-2 mg, once weekly
- No-DAC: 100-200 mcg, 1-3x daily

A buyer who receives DAC when they ordered no-DAC will overdose on every injection. A buyer who receives no-DAC when they ordered DAC will underdose on every injection. Mass spectrometry is required to definitively distinguish between them (DAC adds ~530 Da to molecular weight).

**2. Blend Ratio Issues**

Pre-mixed CJC-1295/Ipamorelin blends present a specific analytical challenge. Because the two compounds behave differently on HPLC columns (different retention times, peak shapes, gradient responses) and ionize differently in mass spectrometry, a blend labeled "99% pure" without separate verification for each component can mask significant imbalances. One peptide can dominate while the other falls well below expected concentration.

**3. Common Impurities**

| Compound | Typical Impurities |
|----------|-------------------|
| CJC-1295 | Deamidation products, truncated sequences, oxidation artifacts, DPP-4-susceptible fragments |
| CJC-1295 DAC | All of the above + hydrolyzed DAC linker variants |
| Ipamorelin | Racemized D-amino acid variants, deletion peptides |

**4. Counterfeiting and Underdosing**

A 2018 study by Janvier et al. (published in *Talanta*) profiled the ten most commonly falsified peptide drugs seized on the Belgian market. Both CJC-1295 and ipamorelin made the list. Industry estimates suggest approximately 95% of online peptides are underdosed, mislabeled, or contaminated (though this figure is vendor-sourced and not independently verified).

### Testing Methods and COA Verification

**What a real COA should include:**

| Test | Method | What It Confirms |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Identity | Mass spectrometry (ESI-MS or MALDI-TOF) | Correct molecular weight: CJC-1295 = 3,367.9 Da; Ipamorelin = 711.85 Da |
| Purity | HPLC with UV detection (214 nm) | Total peptide purity >95% (ideally >99%) |
| Peptide content | Amino acid analysis or nitrogen determination | Actual peptide weight vs. total powder weight (includes salts, moisture) |
| Sterility | USP <71> sterility test | No microbial growth |
| Endotoxin | LAL test (USP <85>) | <5 EU/kg body weight per dose |
| For blends | Separate HPLC peaks for each compound | Correct ratio of CJC-1295 to ipamorelin |
| For DAC | High-resolution MS | Intact maleimido group (not hydrolyzed) |

**Janoshik Analytical** is the most widely referenced third-party testing lab in the peptide community:
- HPLC testing for CJC-1295 (DAC and no-DAC) and ipamorelin: $180 per analysis
- Public verification portal available for result verification
- Many reputable vendors now require Janoshik COAs no older than 3 months

**Red flags on a COA:**
- No chromatogram included (just a number)
- Purity stated as a single number without showing impurity peaks
- No molecular weight confirmation via mass spec
- COA from an unidentifiable laboratory
- Same COA reused across multiple batch numbers
- No lot/batch number matching your specific vial

## 6. Regulatory Status (March 2026)

### FDA Status

| Event | Date | Status |
|-------|------|--------|
| 19 peptides placed on Category 2 list | September 2023 | CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin included -- compounding banned |
| Category 2 nomination withdrawn | September 2024 | CJC-1295 and Ipamorelin (plus AOD-9604, Thymosin Alpha-1, Selank) removed from Category 2 by nominator withdrawal; PCAC voted against inclusion |
| HHS Secretary Kennedy announces reclassification | February 27, 2026 | ~14 of 19 peptides to be returned to Category 1 (announced on Joe Rogan #2461) |
| **Current legal status** | March 2026 | **Neither Category 1 nor Category 2. Regulatory limbo.** No formal FDA rulemaking published. Compounding pharmacies face ambiguous legal exposure. |

**Key clarification:** CJC-1295 and ipamorelin are not FDA-approved drugs. They have never been approved for any therapeutic indication. The Category 1/2 classification determines whether compounding pharmacies can use them as bulk drug substances -- it is not a drug approval pathway.

### WADA Prohibited Status

Both compounds are explicitly prohibited under the 2026 WADA Prohibited List:

- **CJC-1295:** Prohibited under S2.3 as a Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone (GHRH) analog
- **Ipamorelin:** Prohibited under S2.4 as a Growth Hormone Secretagogue (GHS), alongside anamorelin, capromorelin, ibutamoren (MK-677), lenomorelin (ghrelin), macimorelin, and tabimorelin

Both are prohibited at all times (in-competition and out-of-competition). Any athlete subject to WADA testing who uses either compound faces a sanction.

### International Regulatory Status

| Jurisdiction | Status |
|-------------|--------|
| United States | Not FDA-approved; Category status ambiguous; compounding restricted |
| Canada | Not approved by Health Canada; classified as research chemical |
| Australia (TGA) | Schedule 4 (prescription only) for GH secretagogues; strict enforcement |
| United Kingdom (MHRA) | Not licensed for human use; available as research chemical |
| European Union (EMA) | No marketing authorization; enforcement varies by member state |

### Legal Landscape for Consumers

- **Purchasing for personal use:** Gray area. Research-grade vendors operate under "research use only" disclaimers, but major vendors (including Peptide Sciences, the largest) shut down in March 2026 following DOJ prosecutions and FDA warning letters.
- **Prescription via telemedicine:** Legally complex. Clinics prescribe off-label through compounding pharmacies, but the FDA's position that these substances present "significant safety risks" creates legal exposure for both prescribers and pharmacies.
- **Athletic use:** Prohibited by WADA. Detectable via blood/urine testing for GH biomarkers and secretagogue metabolites.
