### 1.3 How to Approach Labs About Affiliate Partnerships

**Pitch framework for Janoshik / Peptide Test / others without existing programs:**

1. **Lead with data:** "Peptide-checker has X monthly visitors specifically researching peptide quality and testing. These are pre-qualified leads."
2. **Propose simple tracking:** Unique coupon codes per partner, UTM parameters, or a simple referral form.
3. **Start with a pilot:** 90-day trial at 15% commission, scaling to 20% at volume thresholds.
4. **Offer editorial value:** "We'll create educational content about why testing matters, naturally driving users toward ordering."
5. **De-risk for the lab:** No upfront cost. Commission-only model means the lab only pays when they earn revenue.

**Email template structure:**
- Subject: "Partnership opportunity: [X] monthly users seeking peptide testing"
- Para 1: Who you are, what peptide-checker does
- Para 2: Your audience demographics and intent signals
- Para 3: Proposed commission structure (15-20%)
- Para 4: What you will provide (content, traffic, tracking)
- Para 5: Ask for a call to discuss

## 2. Affiliate Program Best Practices

### 2.1 Cookie Duration

| Duration | Best For | Conversion Impact |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| 24 hours | Impulse buys (Amazon model) | High conversion rate, misses deliberate buyers |
| 30 days | Standard e-commerce | Industry standard, captures most purchase cycles |
| 45 days | Health testing / considered purchases | Good balance for peptide testing decisions |
| 60-90 days | B2B, high-ticket items | Captures long research cycles |
| 90+ days | Enterprise / SaaS | Best for products requiring multiple stakeholders |

**Recommendation for peptide-checker referrals:** Request 45-60 day cookies from lab partners. Rationale:
- Users discover peptide-checker, research testing options, then may wait for their next peptide order before ordering a test
- Average time from "I should test this" to "I'll order a test" is likely 2-6 weeks
- A SaaS company that increased cookie duration from 30 to 45 days (matching their 38-day average purchase window) saw a **22% jump in affiliate-driven revenue**

### 2.2 Attribution Models

| Model | How It Works | Pros | Cons |
|-------|-------------|------|------|
| **Last-click** | Commission goes to the last referral source before purchase | Simple, standard | Undervalues discovery/education |
| **First-click** | Commission goes to whoever first introduced the customer | Rewards content creators | May over-credit old touchpoints |
| **Linear** | Commission split equally among all touchpoints | Fair | Complex to implement |
| **Time-decay** | More credit to recent touchpoints | Balanced | Requires sophisticated tracking |

**Recommendation:** Negotiate **first-click attribution** with lab partners. Peptide-checker is a discovery/education platform -- users find you first, learn about testing, then may click multiple links before purchasing. First-click ensures peptide-checker gets credited for the initial referral.

### 2.3 FTC Disclosure Requirements (2025-2026)

**Legal requirements:**
- **Must disclose** any material connection between peptide-checker and any testing lab, vendor, or advertiser
- Disclosure must be **"clear and conspicuous"** -- easily noticeable, understandable, not hidden
- Must appear **near the relevant content**, not buried in a footer or separate page
- Must work **across all devices** (mobile, desktop)
- Penalties: Up to **$51,744 per violation** in 2026, with each post/page counted separately

**Disclosure best practices that build trust:**

1. **Top-of-page disclosure on every page with affiliate links:**
   > "Peptide-Checker earns referral commissions when you order testing through our partner labs. This never influences our ratings or recommendations -- we evaluate every vendor identically whether or not we have a financial relationship with the testing lab."

2. **Inline disclosure near each affiliate link:**
   > "Order testing at [Lab Name] (affiliate link -- we earn a commission at no extra cost to you)"

3. **Dedicated "How We Make Money" page** explaining:
   - All revenue streams
   - How editorial independence is maintained
   - That ratings are calculated algorithmically, not influenced by partnerships
   - That you test/rate vendors who are NOT affiliate partners the same as those who are

4. **What NOT to do:**
   - Don't use vague language ("we may receive compensation")
   - Don't hide disclosures in Terms of Service
   - Don't use tiny font or low-contrast text
   - Don't use popups that can be dismissed before being read

**How to disclose without destroying trust:**
- Frame it as **transparency, not apology**: "Here's exactly how we keep the lights on"
- Show that **non-partner vendors get the same ratings treatment**
- Publish your **rating methodology** openly
- Highlight cases where you **rated a partner poorly** or a non-partner highly
- NerdWallet and Wirecutter both prove that clear disclosure actually INCREASES trust because users respect honesty

### 2.4 Affiliate Link Implementation

**Technical stack:**
- **UTM parameters** for Google Analytics tracking: `?utm_source=peptidechecker&utm_medium=affiliate&utm_campaign=testing`
- **Server-side redirect tracking** (e.g., peptidechecker.com/go/janoshik) for cleaner URLs and centralized link management
- **Unique coupon codes** per lab partner for order-level attribution
- **First-party cookies** (not third-party) to survive browser privacy restrictions
- Use a lightweight affiliate tracking solution like Rewardful, FirstPromoter, or self-hosted with simple database logging

## 4. Vendor Certification Revenue

### 4.1 The 3-Tier Certification Model

| Tier | Monthly Fee | What Vendors Get |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Bronze** ($199/mo) | $2,388/year | Verified badge on listing, quarterly re-testing (1 product), basic analytics dashboard, inclusion in "verified vendors" directory |
| **Silver** ($499/mo) | $5,988/year | Everything in Bronze + priority listing in search results, monthly re-testing (up to 3 products), detailed analytics (user clicks, comparison appearances), "Silver Verified" badge with test date |
| **Gold** ($999/mo) | $11,988/year | Everything in Silver + featured placement on homepage, unlimited product re-testing, custom vendor profile page, quarterly published research report on their products, API access to embed ratings on their own site, "Gold Verified" premium badge |

### 4.2 What Makes Vendors Pay

**Value proposition by vendor pain point:**

1. **Trust deficit** -- Gray market peptide vendors struggle to prove quality. A peptide-checker certification badge is a trust signal that directly increases conversions.
2. **Competitive differentiation** -- In a market of 100+ vendors, certification creates visible separation from uncertified competitors.
3. **Reduced customer acquisition cost** -- Vendors currently spend $50-200 per customer acquisition on ads. If peptide-checker sends 50 customers/month via the verified directory, $199-999/month is far cheaper.
4. **Re-testing removes liability** -- Ongoing testing protects vendors from selling degraded product. One bad batch publicly exposed could destroy their business.

### 4.3 Preventing Pay-to-Play Perception

**Critical structural safeguards:**

1. **Separate ratings from certification entirely:**
   - Ratings are algorithmic, based on test results, and applied identically to ALL vendors
   - Certification is a separate "verified" badge that means "this vendor pays for ongoing independent testing"
   - A certified vendor with bad test results STILL gets a bad rating
   - Publish examples of this happening

2. **Transparent methodology:**
   - Publish the exact formula used for ratings
   - Show raw test data alongside computed scores
   - Allow public scrutiny of the methodology

3. **Include non-paying vendors:**
   - Rate ALL vendors in the market, not just those who pay
   - Non-paying vendors can still appear with community-submitted test results
   - This proves the platform isn't gatekeeping

4. **Independent testing oversight:**
   - Certification testing is conducted by third-party labs (Janoshik, Peptide Test), NOT by peptide-checker
   - Peptide-checker never handles samples or influences results
   - Lab selection is randomized or predetermined, not vendor-chosen

5. **Public audit trail:**
   - Every certification test result is published in full
   - Historical test results remain visible even if a vendor cancels certification
   - Failed tests are published immediately, not suppressed

### 4.4 Clean Label Project Cautionary Tale

**What happened:**
- Clean Label Project (CLP), a nonprofit, tested supplement products, issued grades, and offered paid "Purity Awards" and "Transparency Awards"
- In 2020, Pharmavite (maker of Nature Made vitamins) sued CLP under RICO (Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act)
- **Allegation:** CLP sent demand letters requesting **$1.9 million** from Pharmavite for certification to avoid a lawsuit
- CLP allegedly filed **15+ lawsuits** against companies that refused to pay
- Pharmavite proved that contaminant levels CLP flagged were actually **lower than California's Prop 65 safe harbor limits** and comparable to CLP's own "best-rated" products

**Lessons for peptide-checker:**

| CLP Mistake | Peptide-Checker Safeguard |
|------------|--------------------------|
| Threatened lawsuits against non-payers | NEVER threaten or coerce vendors. Certification is optional. |
| Inconsistent testing standards | Publish methodology. Apply identically to all. |
| Revenue dependent on certification fees from the companies being rated | Diversify revenue so certification is ONE stream, not the primary one |
| No published COI policy | Publish a detailed conflict of interest policy on day one |
| Nonprofit status used as credibility shield while running a for-profit racket | Be transparent about being a for-profit business with a public-benefit mission |
| Demand letters for millions | Set affordable, transparent pricing. $199-999/month is reasonable for ongoing testing. |

**Also note the Finnrick parallel:** Finnrick Analytics faces similar criticism -- VC-funded, generates revenue from vendors it rates, no published COI policy, "Private Testing" that allows suppressing bad results. Peptide-checker must be the anti-Finnrick: radical transparency, no private testing suppression, published COI policy.

## 5. Sponsored Content and Advertising

### 5.1 Should Peptide-Checker Accept Advertising?

**Arguments against (strong):**
- Undermines credibility as an independent testing/rating platform
- Creates perceived conflicts of interest even if editorial is genuinely independent
- Peptide vendors advertising on a site that rates them is an obvious conflict
- Display ads degrade user experience, especially on data-heavy comparison pages

**Arguments for (conditional):**
- Relevant, non-vendor advertising (e.g., syringes, BAC water, general health) is less conflicted
- At scale (100K+ monthly visitors), advertising revenue can be significant
- Can supplement other revenue streams without replacing them

**Recommendation:** DO NOT accept vendor advertising in the first 2 years. Build credibility first. After establishing trust, consider:
- Non-vendor display ads only (general health, lifestyle)
- Clearly labeled sponsored sections, physically separated from ratings content
- Never allow advertising to influence ratings placement or visibility

### 5.2 Sponsored Research Reports

**Ethical framework:**
1. Sponsored reports must be **clearly labeled** as sponsored at the top
2. Methodology must be **identical** to non-sponsored reports
3. Sponsor has **no editorial control** over findings or conclusions
4. Negative findings about the sponsor's products are published without modification
5. Reports are reviewed by the editorial team before sponsor review
6. Sponsor can **choose not to publish** but cannot edit content

**Potential sponsors:** Compounding pharmacies, telehealth platforms, research chemical suppliers who want market analysis on peptide quality trends.

**Pricing:** $2,000-$10,000 per sponsored research report depending on scope and distribution.

## 6. Premium API Access

- `/vendors/{id}/rating` -- Current vendor rating and confidence score

                      -------

### 7.5 Critical Success Factors

1. **Editorial independence is the product.** The moment peptide-checker is perceived as pay-to-play, all revenue streams collapse. Protect credibility above all else.

2. **Lab referrals are the bootstrapping engine.** They require zero upfront investment, have 100% margins, and validate that users trust your recommendations.

3. **Subscriptions create defensibility.** Paying subscribers have higher switching costs and provide predictable revenue that investors and partners value.

4. **Certifications scale with the market.** As the peptide market grows, more vendors will compete for trust signals. Certification becomes more valuable as more vendors participate.

5. **API is the long-term moat.** Once telehealth platforms integrate peptide-checker data, switching costs are enormous. This is where the real enterprise value lives.

6. **Never let any single revenue stream exceed 50% of total.** Diversification prevents dependency and protects against market changes.
