## 1. What Is PT-141/Bremelanotide?

### Mechanism of Action

Bremelanotide is a **melanocortin receptor 4 (MC4R) agonist** and synthetic cyclic heptapeptide analogue of alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (alpha-MSH). Per ChEMBL mechanism data:

- **Primary target:** MC4R (CHEMBL259) — expressed on neurons in the central nervous system
- **Action type:** Agonist (direct interaction confirmed)
- **Receptor selectivity order (potency):** MC1R > MC4R > MC3R > MC5R > MC2R
- **Therapeutically relevant targets at approved doses:** MC1R and MC4R

The critical distinction from drugs like Viagra/Cialis: **bremelanotide works centrally in the brain, not peripherally on blood vessels.** It activates presynaptic MC4Rs on neurons in the medial preoptic area (mPOA) of the hypothalamus, leading to increased dopamine release. This modulates desire and arousal through CNS signaling rather than vasodilation.

**Pharmacokinetics:**
- Route: Subcutaneous injection
- Tmax: ~1 hour post-injection
- Elimination half-life: 2.7 hours
- Blood pressure returns to baseline within ~12 hours

### FDA Approval (Vyleesi)

- **Approved:** June 21, 2019
- **Indication:** Treatment of acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women
- **Manufacturer:** Originally AMAG Pharmaceuticals (licensed from Palatin Technologies); transferred back to Palatin in July 2020; sold to Cosette Pharmaceuticals in late 2023
- **Dosage form:** 1.75 mg/0.3 mL single-dose prefilled autoinjector
- **Dosing:** Self-administered SC injection at least 45 minutes before anticipated sexual activity; no more than once in 24 hours; no more than 8 doses per month

### How It Differs from Other Peptides

PT-141/Vyleesi stands alone in the peptide world for several reasons:

1. **Full FDA Phase 4 approval** — Not just "researched" or "studied," but through the complete NDA process with Phase 3 trials
2. **First-in-class** — No other melanocortin receptor agonist approved for sexual dysfunction
3. **CNS mechanism** — Acts on brain pathways (desire) rather than peripheral (erection/blood flow)
4. **Consumer-facing brand** — Marketed directly to patients with TV/print advertising
5. **Parallel gray market** — FDA-approved product coexists with robust research peptide sales

### Relationship to Melanotan II (MT-II)

PT-141 is a **metabolic derivative of Melanotan II** (MT-II). The development path:

1. **Alpha-MSH** — Natural melanocortin hormone
2. **Melanotan II** — Synthetic cyclic analogue of alpha-MSH, originally developed at University of Arizona for sunless tanning
3. **Discovery of sexual side effects** — During MT-II clinical trials, researchers observed unexpected pro-sexual effects (erections, arousal)
4. **PT-141 created** — Deaminated (C-terminal amide group removed) derivative of MT-II, designed to maximize sexual effects while minimizing tanning/melanocyte stimulation
5. **Key structural difference** — PT-141 lacks the C-terminal amide group present in MT-II, reducing MC1R-mediated tanning activity while preserving MC4R-mediated sexual effects

**Critical distinction for consumers:** PT-141 does not cause meaningful skin tanning because it has reduced MC1R activity compared to MT-II. They share structural ancestry but have different pharmacological profiles.

## 2. Clinical Evidence

### RECONNECT Phase 3 Trials

Two identical Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter trials:

**Trial Design:**
- **Study 301:** NCT02338960 — 714 patients enrolled, 91 sites
- **Study 302:** NCT02333071 — separate identical trial
- **Total population:** 1,247 premenopausal women with acquired, generalized HSDD
- **Duration:** 24-week core phase + optional 52-week open-label extension
- **Dose:** Bremelanotide 1.75 mg SC vs. placebo, as-needed
- **Randomization:** 1:1

**Co-Primary Endpoints:**

1. **FSFI Desire Domain (FSFI-D) Score** — Change from baseline to end of study
   - Study 301: +0.30 vs. placebo (P<0.001)
   - Study 302: +0.42 vs. placebo (P<0.001)
   - Integrated: +0.35 vs. placebo (P<0.001)
   - Reported as **2.6x increase in sexual desire vs. placebo**
   - Baseline: 2.06 for both groups (scale 1.2-6.0)
   - Statistically significant at all monthly assessments through Month 6

2. **FSDS-DAO Item 13** (distress related to low sexual desire)
   - Study 301: -0.37 vs. placebo (P<0.001)
   - Study 302: -0.29 vs. placebo (P=0.005)
   - Integrated: -0.33 vs. placebo (P<0.001)
   - Reported as **1.75x decrease in distress vs. placebo**
   - Baseline: 2.9 for both groups (scale 0-4)

**Secondary Endpoint — Satisfying Sexual Events (SSE):**
- **No statistically significant difference** between Vyleesi and placebo
- Note: FDA no longer requires SSE as a coprimary endpoint for HSDD, recognizing that desire and frequency are not the same construct

**Interpretation:**
The efficacy is **statistically significant but modest in absolute terms.** The 0.35-point improvement on a 1.2-6.0 scale translates to a meaningful-but-not-dramatic clinical effect. Nearly 80% of women completing the core phase elected to continue into the open-label extension, suggesting perceived benefit outweighed side effects for most.

### Side Effects Profile

The side effect profile is significant and likely contributed to poor commercial performance:

| Side Effect | Bremelanotide | Placebo |
|---|---|---|
| **Nausea** | **40%** | 1% |
| **Flushing** | **20.3%** | 1.3% |
| **Headache** | **11.3%** | 1.9% |
| **Injection site reactions** | **5.4%** | 0.5% |

**Nausea Details:**
- Median onset: ~1 hour post-dose
- Median duration: ~2 hours
- Worst with first injection; often attenuates with subsequent uses
- Discontinuation rate due to nausea: **8.1%** (meaning ~32% of those who experienced nausea continued using the drug)

**Blood Pressure Effects:**
- Transient increase: +6 mmHg systolic, +3 mmHg diastolic (peaks 2-4 hours post-dose)
- Heart rate decrease: up to 5 bpm
- Returns to baseline within 12 hours
- **Contraindicated** in uncontrolled hypertension or known cardiovascular disease

**Skin Hyperpigmentation:**
- Focal hyperpigmentation reported in areas with dark skin (face, gingiva, breasts)
- Generally resolves after discontinuation but may not fully resolve in all patients

**Dosing Restrictions (from label):**
- Maximum 1 dose per 24 hours
- Maximum 8 doses per month
- Cardiovascular risk assessment required before and periodically during treatment

### Off-Label Uses and Ongoing Research

**Male Erectile Dysfunction (ED):**
- Phase 2 study initiated: Bremelanotide co-formulated with a PDE5 inhibitor (e.g., sildenafil/tadalafil) as a single injection for ED patients non-responsive to PDE5i monotherapy
- Targets the ~30-40% of men who fail Viagra/Cialis
- Phase 3 anticipated first half of 2025 (pending FDA IND acceptance)
- Off-label use already occurring through compounding pharmacies and clinics

**Sexual Medicine Clinic Data:**
- Published abstracts at the Journal of Sexual Medicine (2024) report real-world use of bremelanotide in men with sexual dysfunction at sexual medicine clinics, suggesting growing off-label adoption

**International Development:**
- **China (Fosun Pharma):** First commercial sale in Hainan Province, August 2023
- **South Korea (Kwangdong Pharmaceuticals):** Phase 3 bridging study completed (193 patients, 93% completion rate); regulatory submission to Korean MFDS expected

### Post-Marketing Safety

- Liver enzyme monitoring no longer required (was an early concern during development)
- No new unexpected safety signals reported post-approval
- Long-term extension study data support consistent safety profile over 52+ weeks

## 4. Quality and Testing Considerations

### Stability Profile

PT-141/bremelanotide has specific stability vulnerabilities relevant to quality assessment:

**Light Sensitivity:**
- Bremelanotide is **photosensitive** — exposure to light accelerates degradation
- Must be stored protected from light at all times
- Tryptophan residue in the sequence is particularly vulnerable to photo-oxidation

**Oxidation Vulnerability:**
- Contains oxidation-susceptible residues (tryptophan, methionine)
- Oxidized degradation products may have altered receptor binding
- Storage under nitrogen/argon headspace improves stability

**Temperature Stability:**
- Lyophilized: Stable 3+ years at -20C; ~2 years at 2-8C; 3-4 months at room temp
- Reconstituted: Maximum 30 days at 2-8C
- Freeze-thaw cycles accelerate degradation — avoid frost-free freezers

**Cyclic Structure Advantage:**
- The lactam cyclization provides greater conformational stability than linear peptides
- More resistant to enzymatic degradation than linear analogues
- However, the ring can still open under harsh conditions (extreme pH, heat)

### Testing Considerations for Quality Assessment

**Identity Confirmation:**
- HPLC retention time matching to reference standard
- Mass spectrometry (expected MW: 1025.18 Da free base)
- Amino acid analysis to confirm sequence

**Purity Assessment:**
- RP-HPLC purity (pharmaceutical grade: >98%; research grade: typically >95%)
- Key impurities to monitor:
  - Linear (ring-opened) bremelanotide
  - Oxidized species (Trp oxidation, Met oxidation)
  - Deletion sequences
  - Residual solvents (TFA, acetonitrile)

**Distinguishing Real PT-141 from Counterfeits:**
- Certificate of Analysis (CoA) with third-party verification
- Mass spec confirmation of correct molecular weight
- HPLC showing single major peak with stated purity
- Vendor reputation and testing transparency

**Red Flags in Research Peptides:**
- No CoA available or CoA from unknown lab
- Purity below 95%
- Unusual appearance (PT-141 should be white to off-white lyophilized powder)
- Sold with syringes/bacteriostatic water (signals intent for human use)
- Therapeutic claims on product page

## 5. Regulatory Status

### FDA Approval Status

- **Approved (NDA 210557):** June 21, 2019
- **Indication:** Acquired, generalized HSDD in premenopausal women
- **Not approved for:** Men, postmenopausal women, situational HSDD
- **Black box warning:** None
- **REMS:** None required

### Compounding Pharmacy Status (2026 Update)

The regulatory landscape for compounded PT-141 has undergone significant shifts:

**Timeline:**
1. **Pre-2023:** PT-141/bremelanotide compounded freely at 503A and 503B pharmacies under physician prescription
2. **Late 2023:** FDA moved 19 peptides (including some related compounds) to Category 2, restricting compounding
3. **February 27, 2026:** HHS Secretary Kennedy announced 14 of 19 restricted peptides moving back to Category 1
4. **Current status:** Bremelanotide is eligible for compounding at licensed pharmacies with a physician's prescription

**Key distinction:** Compounded bremelanotide requires a prescription and must use pharmaceutical-grade API (not research-grade or food-grade). The "research use only" loophole does not apply to compounding for human use.

### Research Peptide Market Enforcement

- FDA has increased enforcement against research peptide vendors making therapeutic claims
- June 2025: Amino Asylum warehouse raided by FDA in Memphis, Tennessee
- Vendors selling PT-141 as "research only" with proper labeling generally operate in a legal gray zone
- Selling with injection supplies or making health claims crosses the line into FDA enforcement territory

### WADA Status

Bremelanotide falls under WADA Section S2 (Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors, Related Substances, and Mimetics). Melanocortin receptor agonists are not explicitly called out by name in the publicly accessible summaries of the 2026 Prohibited List, but the S2 category broadly covers "other substances with similar chemical structure or similar biological effect(s)." Athletes should treat PT-141 as potentially prohibited and check with their anti-doping authority before use.

**Practical note:** PT-141 has no known performance-enhancing effects relevant to sport, but its peptide hormone classification means it may trigger testing alerts.

## 6. Melanotan II Connection

### MT-II Market Overview

Melanotan II remains one of the most widely used research peptides globally, primarily for:
- **Tanning** — MC1R-mediated melanogenesis (the original intended use)
- **Sexual effects** — MC4R-mediated (the "side effect" that led to PT-141)
- **Appetite suppression** — MC4R-mediated (secondary effect)

MT-II has **never been approved by any regulatory agency** for any indication. The FDA explicitly warns against tanning injections.

### MT-II Safety Concerns

MT-II carries significantly greater safety risks than PT-141:

**Melanoma and Skin Cancer Risk:**
- Stimulates melanocyte activity via MC1R — increases melanin production
- Case reports of melanoma in MT-II users (causation debated; confounded by UV-seeking behavior)
- Darkens existing moles and freckles, creates new nevi
- **Makes melanoma detection harder** by altering the appearance of moles
- A 2021 review concluded increased melanoma risk in MT-II users "can probably be explained by more UV exposure" (users seek sun/beds to activate tanning)

**Other Serious Risks:**
- Rhabdomyolysis (muscle breakdown → kidney damage)
- Priapism (dangerous prolonged erections requiring emergency treatment)
- Kidney dysfunction
- Reports of brain swelling (rare)
- Nausea, facial flushing, abdominal cramps (common)

### How MT-II and PT-141 Differ

| Feature | Melanotan II | PT-141 (Bremelanotide) |
|---|---|---|
| **FDA Status** | Not approved, never will be | Approved (Vyleesi, 2019) |
| **Structure** | Cyclic heptapeptide with C-terminal amide | Same core, C-terminal amide removed |
| **Primary MC1R activity** | High (tanning) | Reduced |
| **MC4R activity** | High (sexual effects) | High (sexual effects) |
| **Tanning effect** | Yes — significant | Minimal to none |
| **Melanoma concern** | Yes — mole changes, new nevi | Focal hyperpigmentation only |
| **Clinical trials** | Minimal, never progressed | Full Phase 3 program |
| **Safety data** | Case reports only | Controlled trial data on 1,247+ women |
