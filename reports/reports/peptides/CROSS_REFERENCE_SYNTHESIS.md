## Overarching Thesis

The four completed peptide reports converge on a single conclusion: **the consumer peptide market has a quality verification crisis that is structurally worse than most consumers realize, but the tools to address it are becoming more accessible.**

The quality problem (PEP-001) is not just about "bad vendors" -- it is compounded by degradation that consumers cannot detect (PEP-004), a regulatory environment that pushes products to unregulated channels (PEP-002), and testing methods that remain too expensive for routine use but are trending toward affordability (PEP-003).

## Cross-Cutting Findings

### 1. The Purity Numbers Are Misleading Without Context

**PEP-001 + PEP-003 + PEP-004:** HPLC purity percentages (the standard metric on COAs) tell only part of the story.

- PEP-001 reveals that BPC-157's Asp-Asp motif generates aspartimide isomers that mass spectrometry cannot distinguish from correct peptide. Standard HPLC may not resolve them either without optimized gradients.
- PEP-003 confirms that the best consumer-accessible test (LC-MS at $200) combines HPLC and MS, but even this combo has blind spots for isobaric impurities.
- PEP-004 shows that degradation products (deamidation, oxidation) accumulate post-reconstitution and may not appear on the original vendor COA, which was tested on freshly synthesized material.

**Implication:** A vendor COA showing 99% purity is a necessary but insufficient quality signal. It tells you the peptide was pure when tested, not that it is pure when you inject it. Time-since-reconstitution and storage conditions matter as much as initial purity.

### 2. Endotoxin Contamination Is the Most Underappreciated Risk

**PEP-001 + PEP-002 + PEP-003:** All three reports flag endotoxin contamination as a critical and widespread problem.

- PEP-001: 8% of gray-market BPC-157 samples show measurable endotoxin; 65% of online peptides in one study exceeded safety thresholds.
- PEP-002: 100% of online-sold semaglutide samples in the JMIR study had endotoxin contamination (2.16-8.95 EU/mg).
- PEP-003: Endotoxin testing (LAL assay) is available as an add-on at Janoshik ($20-240) but is not included in standard consumer testing panels.

**Implication:** Most consumers testing their peptides will check purity (HPLC) and identity (MS) but not endotoxin levels. Yet endotoxin contamination may be the single most dangerous quality failure for injectable peptides, causing fever, chills, or septic shock. The $200 LC-MS test does not cover endotoxin.

### 3. The Regulatory Squeeze Creates a Quality Paradox

**PEP-001 + PEP-002:** FDA actions are simultaneously protecting consumers and pushing them toward worse options.

- PEP-001: BPC-157's Category 2 classification bars licensed compounding pharmacies from making it, leaving only the gray market.
- PEP-002: The GLP-1 compounding crackdown (50+ warning letters, Import Alert 66-80) is shutting down compounders -- but compounders, however imperfect, were a step above raw "research-grade" peptide vendors. With compounders exiting, price-sensitive consumers may shift to even less regulated sources.

**Implication:** Enforcement without accessible alternatives does not eliminate demand; it pushes it underground. The Novo/Hims settlement (PEP-002) is a model where enforcement paired with expanded brand access can actually improve consumer outcomes. No such resolution exists for peptides like BPC-157 that lack FDA-approved equivalents.

### 4. Storage and Handling Are Consumer-Controllable Variables

**PEP-004 + PEP-001:** Even a high-purity peptide from a top vendor degrades if handled poorly.

- PEP-004: Reconstituted BPC-157 degrades via hydrolysis, oxidation, and aggregation. BAC water extends useful life from 1-2 weeks (sterile water) to 4-6 weeks. Each freeze-thaw cycle costs TB-500 2-5% content.
- PEP-001: Quantity accuracy varies +/-75% across vendors. Combined with degradation losses, the actual dose delivered to the user may differ dramatically from what they calculate.
- PEP-004: Moisture is more destructive than moderate temperature excursions. A compromised vial seal exposed to 70% humidity for 24 hours causes more damage than an intact vial at 45C for 48 hours.

**Implication:** Consumer education on storage (use BAC water, refrigerate immediately, wrap vials in foil, check seal integrity, aliquot before freezing) can partially compensate for the quality variance in the supply chain. This is a high-leverage, low-cost intervention.

### 5. Testing Economics Are Improving But Still Inaccessible for Routine Use

**PEP-003 + PEP-001:** The cost structure of peptide testing creates a catch-22.

- PEP-003: LC-MS testing has dropped from $500+ to $200 per sample. Three consumer-facing labs now accept direct mail-in samples.
- PEP-001: Budget peptides cost $10-25 per 5mg vial. A $200 test on a $20 vial is economically irrational for individual consumers.

**Implication:** Community-funded batch testing (where groups pool resources to test vendors collectively) is the most viable path to affordable quality assurance. Finnrick's model, despite its conflicts of interest, demonstrates that centralized testing-and-rating systems can influence vendor behavior. A more transparent, community-governed version of this model would significantly improve the landscape.

## Cross-Cutting Finding 6: The Regulatory Announcement-to-Implementation Gap Creates Maximum Consumer Risk

**PEP-005 + PEP-001 + PEP-002:** The Kennedy reclassification announcement (Feb 27, 2026) created demand before any quality infrastructure exists.

- PEP-005: The announcement was made on a podcast (Joe Rogan #2461), not through formal FDA rulemaking. No Federal Register notice, no amended statute, no official list of the 14 peptides. As of March 2026, all 19 peptides technically remain Category 2.
- PEP-001: The gray market is simultaneously collapsing (Peptide Sciences shut down Mar 6, 2026; 7+ vendors closed in 2025).
- PEP-002: The GLP-1 compounding crackdown shows what happens when enforcement removes one supply channel without a functional alternative.

**Implication:** Consumers hearing "peptides are becoming legal" may rush to purchase from the remaining (and potentially worst) gray-market vendors, during the exact period when the best vendors are shutting down. This announcement-to-implementation gap is arguably the highest-risk window for consumer harm.

### 7. WADA Prohibition Is Independent and Permanent for Athletes

**PEP-005:** Even if all 14 peptides are reclassified to Category 1, WADA prohibition remains completely independent. A valid prescription does NOT provide a WADA exemption. Athletes need a Therapeutic Use Exemption (TUE), which is extremely difficult to obtain for performance-related peptides. GHK-Cu is a notable exception (not WADA-listed).

**Implication for Peptide Checker:** Any consumer-facing tool must display WADA status prominently alongside FDA status, as the two regulatory systems are completely independent.

### 8. The Salt Form Problem Is a Hidden Quality Marker

**PEP-002 (extended) + PEP-001:** Compounders using semaglutide sodium or semaglutide acetate instead of semaglutide base creates unknown pharmacokinetic differences. Combined with the counterion issue in PEP-001 (TFA vs acetate salt in BPC-157 affecting actual peptide content by up to 35%), salt form is a consistently under-examined quality variable.

**Implication:** A peptide verification service should flag salt form discrepancies as a testable, actionable quality marker.
