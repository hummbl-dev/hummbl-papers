## 1. Executive Summary

**Peptide Checker** is a consumer-facing verification platform that helps people determine whether the peptides they are purchasing or considering are safe, authentic, and appropriately sourced. It combines a vendor quality database, COA (Certificate of Analysis) verification tools, regulatory status tracking, and consumer education into a single trusted resource.

**Who it is for:** The estimated 2-5 million Americans actively purchasing research peptides or compounded GLP-1 agonists, plus the broader population seeking peptide therapy through legitimate compounding pharmacies as the Category 2 reclassification takes effect.

**Why now:** Five converging forces create an unprecedented market window:

1. **The quality crisis is documented and severe.** The JMIR study found online semaglutide at 7-14% purity (vs. 99% claimed). Finnrick data shows 60% of BPC-157 vendors earn D or E ratings. 900+ FAERS adverse event reports and 17 deaths are linked to compounded GLP-1 products.

2. **The gray market is collapsing while legitimate access is not yet open.** Peptide Sciences (the largest vendor) shut down March 6, 2026. Seven-plus vendors closed in 2025. The Kennedy reclassification (announced Feb 27, 2026) promises Category 1 access for ~14 peptides, but no formal FDA rule has been published. This interstitial period -- gray market gone, legitimate market not yet operational -- is the highest-risk window for consumer harm.

3. **FDA enforcement is creating an information vacuum.** 55+ warning letters, DOJ prosecutions ($1.79M Tailor Made forfeiture), and pharmaceutical company litigation are clearing the market of vendors -- but consumers still have demand, and they have fewer tools to evaluate remaining sources.

4. **Search demand validates the market.** BPC-157 generates 165,000+ monthly Google searches. GLP-1 agonist interest is at all-time highs, driven by the obesity epidemic and $1,300-$1,900/month branded drug costs.

5. **Testing costs are dropping.** Consumer-accessible HPLC testing has fallen from $500+ to $7-$200 per sample. Three consumer-facing labs now accept direct mail-in samples. The infrastructure for verification is becoming affordable.

**The opportunity:** Build the "Consumer Reports for peptides" -- an independent, trusted verification layer between consumers and a fragmented, under-regulated market. Monetize through freemium tools, vendor certification, testing lab referrals, and subscription services.

**TAM (Total Addressable Market):** The peptide verification and quality assurance market serves the intersection of a $25B+ branded drug market and a multi-billion-dollar compounded/gray-market segment. Conservative TAM for a verification service: $50-200M annually in the US.

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):** Consumers actively seeking third-party verification or vendor evaluation: $10-50M annually.

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market, Year 1-2):** $100K-$500K, achievable through freemium tools with premium upsells and lab referral commissions.

### 2.2 Consumer Pain Points

1. **No reliable way to evaluate vendors.** 60% of tested vendors earn D/E ratings (Finnrick). Vendor-provided COAs are unreliable -- the JMIR study showed 99% claimed purity vs. 7-14% actual.

2. **Endotoxin contamination is invisible and dangerous.** 100% of JMIR semaglutide samples contained endotoxin. 8% of gray-market BPC-157 samples show measurable endotoxin. 65% of online peptides in one study exceeded endotoxin safety thresholds. Standard $200 consumer testing does not include endotoxin.

3. **Regulatory confusion.** The Kennedy announcement created the impression that "peptides are legal again," but no formal FDA rule has changed. Consumers cannot determine what is legally available in their state.

4. **Price-quality uncertainty.** Budget peptides ($10-25/5mg) are high-risk, but high price does not guarantee quality either. Several D/E-rated vendors charge mid-range prices.

5. **Storage destroys quality invisibly.** Reconstituted BPC-157 degrades within days at room temperature. Each freeze-thaw cycle costs TB-500 2-5% content. Consumers lack guidance on proper handling.

6. **Testing is economically irrational for individuals.** A $200 test on a $20 vial makes no sense. Community-funded batch testing is the only viable model, but no coordinated platform exists.

### 2.3 Regulatory Tailwinds

- **FDA enforcement creates demand for verification.** As illegitimate vendors are removed, consumers need tools to distinguish remaining quality sources.
- **Category 1 reclassification will expand the legitimate compounding market.** Verification shifts from "is this real?" to "is this pharmacy compliant?" -- a larger and more sustainable market.
- **State AG enforcement is accelerating.** 38+ state AGs have formally demanded federal action. Connecticut, Alabama, New York, and California are leading enforcement. This validates the problem and creates media attention that drives consumer awareness.
- **Telehealth integration opportunity.** As compounded peptides become legally available again, telehealth platforms will need quality verification partners.

## 3. Product Roadmap (Phased)

### Phase 1: MVP (Months 1-3) -- "Trust Layer"

**Core deliverables:**
- **Vendor quality database** -- Expand existing peptide_db.py (currently 30 vendors, 2 peptides) to a web-accessible database aggregating Finnrick, Janoshik, and community-reported data across all major peptides.
- **COA verification tool** -- Upload a vendor COA; the tool checks for red flags (round purity numbers, missing chromatograms, inconsistent date formats, known fabricated documents).
- **Regulatory status tracker** -- Real-time status of all 19 Category 2 peptides (FDA status, WADA status, state-level enforcement).
- **Consumer education hub** -- Expand existing consumer_guide.md into a searchable, peptide-by-peptide reference with evidence tiers, clinical trial status, and safety information.
- **Storage calculator** -- Input peptide type, reconstitution date, diluent, and storage temperature; receive estimated remaining potency and discard date.

**Technical stack:** Python backend (FastAPI), SQLite/PostgreSQL, simple web frontend. Deploy on Cloudflare Workers or similar low-cost infrastructure.

**Revenue:** Free. Build audience and trust.

### Phase 2: Enhanced Tools (Months 4-6) -- "Verification Engine"

- **AI COA document analyzer** -- OCR + ML model to extract data from uploaded COA images, cross-reference against known lab formats, flag inconsistencies, and compare claimed results against database averages.
- **Testing lab recommender** -- Based on user's peptide, location, budget, and concern type, recommend the optimal testing pathway (Finnrick free HPLC vs. Janoshik full panel vs. Peptide Test $7 screen).
- **Vendor alerts** -- Email/push notifications when a tracked vendor's rating changes, new test data is published, or regulatory action is taken.
- **WADA athlete module** -- Dedicated section for athletes with WADA prohibition status, TUE guidance, and detection window information.

**Revenue:** Freemium. Free basic access; $9.99/month subscription for alerts, unlimited COA scans, and detailed vendor reports.

### Phase 3: Testing Service Partnership (Months 7-12) -- "Verification Network"

- **Mail-in testing concierge** -- Partnership with Finnrick, Janoshik, and Peptide Test to offer seamless sample submission through the Peptide Checker interface. Handle logistics, payment, and result interpretation.
- **Community batch testing program** -- Coordinate group buys for testing. Users pool funds ($5-10 each) to test a specific vendor/batch. Results published to the database.
- **Vendor certification program** -- Vendors pay for ongoing independent testing and earn a "Peptide Checker Verified" badge. Tiered program: Bronze (HPLC quarterly), Silver (HPLC + MS quarterly), Gold (full panel + endotoxin monthly).

**Revenue:** Testing service commissions (15-25% per referral); vendor certification fees ($199-$999/month by tier).

### Phase 4: ML-Enhanced Analysis (Months 13-24) -- "Intelligence Platform"

- **HPLC chromatogram analyzer** -- ML model trained on known-good and known-bad chromatograms to detect anomalies, degradation patterns, and fabricated data.
- **Predictive vendor scoring** -- ML model that predicts vendor quality trends based on testing history, batch frequency, COA consistency, and community reports.
- **Degradation predictor** -- Given a peptide's test results, storage conditions, and time since reconstitution, estimate current potency with confidence intervals.
- **Integration with healthcare MCP tools** -- Connect to ICD-10 (diagnosis codes), NPI (provider verification), CMS Coverage (insurance status), Clinical Trials (active studies), and ChEMBL (compound data) for comprehensive consumer decision support.

**Revenue:** B2B API access for telehealth platforms, compounding pharmacies, and healthcare providers ($299-$999/month). Enhanced consumer subscription ($19.99/month).

### Phase 5: At-Home Testing (Years 3-5+) -- "Consumer Hardware"

- **Lateral flow immunoassay development** -- Partner with a diagnostics company to develop rapid yes/no identity tests for high-volume peptides (semaglutide, BPC-157). Estimated development cost: $100K+ per target peptide.
- **Aptamer-based sensor integration** -- As aptamer technology matures (currently TRL 3-5), integrate with consumer-grade detection devices.
- **Smartphone spectroscopy** -- When handheld Raman/NIR devices drop below $500 with adequate sensitivity, build a cloud-based "scan and identify" service with AI-enhanced spectral matching.

**Revenue:** Hardware sales, consumable test strips, cloud analysis subscription.

**Timeline note:** At-home peptide testing hardware is 5-10 years from consumer readiness. This phase is strategic positioning, not near-term revenue.

## 6. Regulatory Strategy

### 6.1 CLIA Certification: Not Required

Peptide Checker is NOT a clinical laboratory. Key distinctions:

- CLIA applies to labs that test "materials derived from the human body" for health assessment or disease diagnosis.
- Peptide verification tests the *product*, not the *patient*.
- Analytical testing of consumer products (like supplement testing) does not require CLIA certification.
- **Confirmed in RQ-PEP-003:** "A peptide testing service does NOT require CLIA certification if it performs non-clinical analytical testing."

### 6.2 ISO 17025 Path (Voluntary but Strategic)

ISO/IEC 17025 is the international standard for testing and calibration laboratories. While not required for Peptide Checker's software platform:

- **Phase 1-2:** Not needed. The platform aggregates data from other labs (Janoshik is ISO 17025 accredited).
- **Phase 3+:** If Peptide Checker operates its own testing or coordinates testing, ISO 17025 accreditation for partner labs should be a requirement for the "Verified" badge program.
- **Credibility benefit:** Referencing ISO 17025-accredited lab partners in all marketing materials builds trust.

### 6.3 State Licensing Considerations

- **No pharmacy license required** -- Peptide Checker does not compound, dispense, or sell peptides.
- **No medical license required** -- The platform provides information and testing referrals, not medical advice.
- **Consumer protection compliance** -- Clear disclaimers on every page: "This is not medical advice. Peptide Checker does not endorse, recommend, or sell any peptide product."
- **State-specific advertising rules** -- Some states (New York, California) have strict rules about health-related advertising. Legal review before launch.

### 6.4 Navigating Reclassification Uncertainty

The Kennedy announcement (Feb 27, 2026) vs. the absence of formal FDA rulemaking creates a unique positioning challenge:

**Strategy:** Be the authoritative source on *what has actually changed* vs. *what has been announced.*

- Track and clearly display: (a) date of announcement, (b) current formal FDA status, (c) expected timeline for rulemaking, (d) what consumers can legally do *today* vs. what they may be able to do *when rules change*.
- Update in real-time as Federal Register notices, PCAC votes, and state actions occur.
- This becomes a moat: the platform that most accurately tracks the regulatory landscape earns the most trust.

**Risk mitigation:** Never imply that a peptide is "legal" or "safe" based on the announcement alone. Always reference the actual formal status.

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

### 9.1 Regulatory Risk: Peptides Get Scheduled

**Risk:** If certain peptides (BPC-157, MK-677, etc.) are placed on DEA schedules, the gray market collapses entirely and consumer demand shifts.

**Probability:** Low (5-10%). The political direction is toward reclassification to Category 1, not scheduling. Scheduling requires a separate DEA process.

**Mitigation:** The platform's value proposition shifts to *legitimate compounding pharmacy verification* -- a larger and more stable market. Also: expand coverage to include FDA-approved peptide drugs (semaglutide, tirzepatide, tesamorelin) where verification of compounding quality remains valuable.

### 9.2 Liability Risk: Consumer Acts on Verification

**Risk:** A consumer uses Peptide Checker data to justify purchasing and injecting a peptide that causes harm. The platform is sued.

**Probability:** Moderate (15-25% over 5 years).

**Mitigation:**
- Comprehensive disclaimers on every page: "Not medical advice. Not an endorsement. Not a recommendation to purchase or use."
- Never recommend specific vendors by name for purchase; only report test data.
- Carry general liability and errors & omissions insurance ($500-$2,000/year).
- Structure as information service, not advisory service.
- Legal review of all consumer-facing content.

### 9.3 Technology Risk: At-Home Testing Delayed

**Risk:** Consumer-grade peptide testing hardware takes longer than 5-10 years to materialize, limiting Phase 5.

**Probability:** High (60-70%). No startups are working on at-home peptide testing devices today.

**Mitigation:** Phase 5 is strategic positioning, not a revenue dependency. The core business (Phases 1-4) generates revenue independent of hardware development. If hardware emerges, Peptide Checker is positioned as the software/data layer.

### 9.4 Market Risk: FDA Enforcement Kills Demand

**Risk:** Aggressive FDA enforcement completely eliminates gray-market peptides and compounded alternatives, reducing the consumer base.

**Probability:** Low-Moderate (15-20%). Even with aggressive enforcement, IQVIA data shows compounded semaglutide prescriptions *increased* after the shortage ended. Price-driven demand is structural.

**Mitigation:** Pivot to compounding pharmacy quality verification (the legitimate market). As reclassification makes peptides legally available through compounding, the verification market grows rather than shrinks -- consumers and prescribers need to evaluate *which pharmacy* to trust.

### 9.5 Competitive Risk: Finnrick or Janoshik Builds a Platform

**Risk:** An existing testing lab builds the aggregation/verification platform that Peptide Checker is building.

**Probability:** Moderate (20-30%).

**Mitigation:**
- First-mover advantage in aggregation across *multiple* data sources (neither Finnrick nor Janoshik aggregates the other's data).
- Regulatory tracking and consumer education are differentiated features that testing labs are unlikely to build.
- Position as the neutral, independent aggregator -- not competing with labs but partnering with them.
- Build network effects: more users = more community testing data = more valuable database = more users.

### 9.6 Data Integrity Risk: Vendor Gaming

**Risk:** Vendors submit cherry-picked samples for testing, manipulate community reviews, or pressure the platform to alter ratings.

**Probability:** High (80%+ will be attempted).

**Mitigation:**
- Blind testing protocols (samples purchased independently, not submitted by vendors).
- Multiple data source triangulation (Finnrick + Janoshik + community + self-collected).
- Transparent methodology documentation.
- Clear conflict-of-interest disclosure policies.
- Vendor certification requires ongoing random testing, not vendor-submitted samples.
